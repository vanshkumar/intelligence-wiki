Jack D. Cowan is a mathematical and theoretical neuroscientist at the University of Chicago whose work spans neural population dynamics, neural field theory, pattern formation in cortex, and — more recently — stochastic and field-theoretic descriptions of neural activity. Trained originally in electrical engineering and information theory (with Warren McCulloch and with Shannon-era computing theorists), Cowan has been a central figure in the application of statistical-physics methods to brain dynamics for more than five decades.

## Selected contributions relevant to this wiki

- [[wilson-cowan-1972-ei-populations|Wilson & Cowan (1972)]] — the two-variable E-I population model that launched the neural-mass-modeling literature. With [[Hugh Wilson]], derived the canonical Wilson-Cowan equations from a refractoriness-times-sigmoid-response picture via temporal coarse-graining; established the three generic dynamical regimes (simple hysteresis, multiple hysteresis, limit cycles) and Theorem 4 on regime interconvertibility by nonspecific biasing inputs.
- **Wilson & Cowan (1973)**. Spatial extension of the 1972 model to a 2D neural field; framework for travelling waves, pattern formation, and spatial hysteresis. Direct ancestor of Ermentrout & Cowan (1979) on geometric visual hallucinations.
- **Ermentrout & Cowan (1979)**. Showed that drug-induced geometric visual hallucinations (tunnels, spirals, lattices) correspond to the linear pattern-formation modes of a Wilson-Cowan-style neural field on V1, mapped through the retina-V1 cortical magnification. A striking instance of visible cortical dynamics read directly from a mean-field model.
- **Statistical mechanics of cortex** (long lineage with colleagues including Bressloff, Buice, and others). Path-integral formulations of stochastic Wilson-Cowan equations, treating mean-field ODEs as the classical limit of a fluctuating field theory over finite populations. This work provides the bridge between mean-field cortical models and the formal apparatus of statistical field theory.
- **Buice & Cowan (2007, 2009)**. Field-theoretic treatment of stochastic Wilson-Cowan dynamics, including critical behavior and fluctuation corrections to mean-field predictions. Relevant to critiques of "criticality in cortex" and to the analysis of neuronal avalanches.
