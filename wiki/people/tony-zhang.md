Tony Zhang is a computational neuroscientist in [[Markus Meister|Meister]]'s lab at Caltech. First author on the endotaxis proposal — a neuromorphic circuit model for cognitive navigation that repurposes the ancient chemotaxis module to operate on internally computed goal signals.

## Relevant Work

- [[zhang-2024-endotaxis-neuromorphic-navigation|Zhang et al. (2024)]] — introduces [[Endotaxis]]: a 3-layer Hebbian circuit (point cells, map cells, goal cells) that computes an internal "virtual odor" monotonic in graph distance, accounting for one-shot homing, few-shot reward learning, and efficient patrolling documented in [[rosenberg-2021-labyrinth-learning|Rosenberg et al. (2021)]].
