Elissa Hallem is a neurobiologist at UCLA whose lab studies chemosensation and host-seeking behavior in nematodes, including free-living *C. elegans* and parasitic species. Her work has established CO₂ chemosensation as a tractable model for sensory circuit function across life stages and species.

## Relevant Work in This Wiki

- [[banerjee-2023-life-stage-chemosensation|Banerjee et al. (2023)]] (senior author with Paul Sternberg) — demonstrated that CO₂ chemotaxis in *C. elegans* is implemented by different microcircuits in starved adults vs. dauer larvae despite identical anatomy and shared behavioral valence. Key case study for [[Context-Dependent Circuit Reconfiguration]].
