Krishna V. Shenoy (1968–2023) was a Stanford neuroengineer whose lab was central to the dynamical-systems framing of motor cortex and to brain-computer interface (BCI) research. His collaborations with Mark Churchland, Matthew Golub, David Sussillo, and many others produced much of the canonical evidence for preparatory activity as initial condition, output-null structure, rotational dynamics, and manifold-constrained learning — the core motifs of [[Computation Through Dynamics|CTD]].
