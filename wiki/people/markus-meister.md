Markus Meister is a neuroscientist at Caltech (Division of Biology and Biological Engineering, and Chrissy Chen Institute for Neuroscience). Known for work on retinal processing and neural coding, with broader interests in learning and information theory applied to behavior.

## Relevant Work

- Author of [[meister-2022-learning-fast-slow|Meister (2022)]], a review arguing that the 10,000× difference in learning rates across tasks is explained by whether the animal has sparse neural codes for the relevant events — not by task complexity or the speed of synaptic plasticity.
