Matthew Rosenberg is a neuroscientist in [[Markus Meister|Meister]]'s lab at Caltech. First author on the overnight labyrinth study that exposed few-shot learning, sudden insight, and intrinsic exploration in mice under unsupervised naturalistic conditions; co-author on the companion [[Endotaxis|endotaxis]] circuit proposal.

## Relevant Work

- [[rosenberg-2021-labyrinth-learning|Rosenberg et al. (2021)]] — mice in a 6-level binary-tree labyrinth exhibit one-shot home-path learning, few-shot reward learning, sudden-insight transitions, and efficient exploration governed by 4 genetically-consistent local turning biases.
- [[zhang-2024-endotaxis-neuromorphic-navigation|Zhang, Rosenberg, Jing, Perona & Meister (2024)]] — mechanistic complement to the 2021 paper: endotaxis circuit that quantitatively accounts for the behavioral phenomena.
