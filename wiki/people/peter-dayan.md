Peter Dayan is a computational neuroscientist and theoretical AI researcher whose work has shaped Bayesian approaches to brain function, reinforcement learning theory, and neural-network models of learning. He co-authored the Helmholtz Machine paper ([[dayan-hinton-1994-helmholtz-machine|Dayan, Hinton, Neal & Zemel 1994]]) while at the University of Toronto, alongside a parallel thread of influential work on dopamine and temporal-difference reward prediction. He is currently director at the Max Planck Institute for Biological Cybernetics in Tübingen; previously founding director of the Gatsby Computational Neuroscience Unit at UCL.

## Themes in his work relevant to this wiki

- **Variational inference and hierarchical generative models in the brain.** Co-developer of the [[Helmholtz Machine]] and [[Wake-Sleep Algorithm]]; early advocate for variational [[Variational Free Energy|free-energy]] approaches to perception and learning, which later inform Friston's [[Active Inference]] and modern hierarchical [[Predictive Coding]].
- **Reinforcement learning and dopamine.** Landmark work (with Read Montague and Terry Sejnowski) on interpreting dopamine neuron firing as a temporal-difference reward prediction error (Montague, Dayan & Sejnowski 1996; Schultz, Dayan & Montague 1997) — foundational for the TD-dopamine hypothesis.
- **Model-based vs. model-free reinforcement learning.** Dayan & Daw's work on how the brain arbitrates between habit (model-free) and planning (model-based) systems; directly relevant to the control thesis that organizes this wiki.
- **Textbook.** Co-author, with Larry Abbott, of *Theoretical Neuroscience* (2001) — a standard graduate-level reference.
