Nikolay Kukushkin is a neurobiologist at NYU (Liberal Studies and the Center for Neural Science, Carew lab). His work concerns the molecular logic of memory formation, particularly the kinetics of the kinase cascades — PKA, PKC, ERK, and downstream CREB — that decode temporally patterned stimulation into long-term transcriptional and synaptic change. He has been a primary developer of the framework that treats the **spacing effect** as a quantitative consequence of cascade dynamics rather than a circuit-level emergent property.

## Themes in his work relevant to this wiki

- **Precision timing of ERK in long-term memory** ([[kukushkin-2024-massed-spaced-non-neural|Kukushkin et al. 2024]]; Kukushkin et al. 2022 *PNAS*). The kinetics of ERK phosphorylation and dephosphorylation set the optimal inter-trial interval for long-term memory induction. The 2024 paper extends this from *Aplysia* sensory neurons to non-neural human cell lines (SH-SY5Y, HEK293), demonstrating that the spacing effect can be reproduced in cells with no neural ancestry.
- **Cellular cognition.** Kukushkin's writing (e.g., *Learn. Mem.* 2018 "Taking memory beyond the brain: Does tobacco mosaic virus dream of the mosaic mouse?") makes the case that canonical features of memory — temporal pattern detection, transcriptional consolidation, decay following Ebbinghaus-style curves — are properties of conserved signaling cascades present in many cell types, not unique to neurons.
- **Pattern detection in TGFβ and other cascades.** Miranda, Mirisis, Asok, Kukushkin & Carew 2023 (*PNAS*) demonstrate pattern-detection dynamics in the TGFβ cascade controlling long-term synaptic plasticity in *Aplysia*. The same logic — cascade kinetics decoding temporal patterns — applied to a different signaling pathway.

## Position relative to other lineages in this wiki

- **Kandel / Carew *Aplysia* lineage.** Kukushkin trained in Carew's lab; his work extends the long *Aplysia*-based program (Kandel, Carew, Pittenger, Bartsch, Tully) on molecular mechanisms of long-term sensitization and facilitation. The 2024 paper is the cell-line abstraction of the *Aplysia* spacing-effect program.
- **Computational cascade modeling.** Smolen, Baxter & Byrne (2008, 2016) and Pagani et al. (2009) provided dynamical-systems models of the spacing effect; Kukushkin's experiments are the empirical complement, with the CRE-luc reporter cell system designed to be tractable for systematic dynamical-systems studies.
- **[[meister-2022-learning-fast-slow|Meister (2022)]] / fast vs slow learning.** Kukushkin's findings supply the *molecular* contribution to slow learning (24-h consolidation kinetics) that complements Meister's *representational* contribution (slow sparse-code construction). Together they suggest that animal learning is slow at multiple levels of analysis.

## Sources in this wiki

- [[kukushkin-2024-massed-spaced-non-neural|Kukushkin, Carney, Tabassum & Carew (2024)]] — "The massed-spaced learning effect in non-neural human cells." *Nature Communications.* Demonstrates the canonical spacing effect in SH-SY5Y and HEK293 cells; ERK and CREB inhibition both block it; PKA encodes duration, PKC encodes event count.
