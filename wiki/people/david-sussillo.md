David Sussillo is a computational neuroscientist whose work has been central to the use of recurrent neural networks as models of neural population dynamics. His contributions include the FORCE learning rule for RNN training, fixed-point analysis methods for trained RNNs (Sussillo & Barak 2013, Golub & Sussillo 2018), and task-trained RNN demonstrations of how low-dimensional oscillators can produce motor patterns (Sussillo et al. 2015) — the sufficiency test for [[Rotational Dynamics]] in motor cortex.

Sussillo's methodological framing — RNNs as hypothesis generators for biological dynamics — threads through much of the CTD literature in this wiki, including the Vyas review and [[brennan-2023-looper-computational-scaffold|Brennan et al. (2023)]]'s monkey–RNN computational scaffold comparison.
