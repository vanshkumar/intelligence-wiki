David Sussillo is a computational neuroscientist whose work has been central to the use of recurrent neural networks as models of neural population dynamics. His contributions include the [[force-learning|FORCE learning]] rule for RNN training ([[sussillo-abbott-2009-force-learning|Sussillo & Abbott 2009]]), fixed-point analysis methods for trained RNNs (Sussillo & Barak 2013, Golub & Sussillo 2018), and task-trained RNN demonstrations of how low-dimensional oscillators can produce motor patterns (Sussillo et al. 2015) — the sufficiency test for [[Rotational Dynamics]] in motor cortex.

Sussillo's methodological framing — RNNs as hypothesis generators for biological dynamics — threads through much of the CTD literature in this wiki, including the Vyas review and [[brennan-2023-looper-computational-scaffold|Brennan et al. (2023)]]'s monkey–RNN computational scaffold comparison.

## Relevant Work in This Wiki

- [[sussillo-abbott-2009-force-learning|Sussillo & Abbott (2009)]] — first-author on the foundational FORCE paper: trains chaotic recurrent networks to produce complex coherent outputs; three architectures; preparation-as-chaos-suppression reading; explicit CTD stance 11 years before the label.
- [[vyas-2020-computation-through-dynamics|Vyas, Golub, Sussillo & Shenoy (2020)]] — coauthor on the CTD review; methodological scaffolding for RNN-as-hypothesis-generator is his contribution.
- Cited as underlying methodology in [[brennan-2023-looper-computational-scaffold|Brennan et al. (2023)]] (RNN dynamics comparison).
