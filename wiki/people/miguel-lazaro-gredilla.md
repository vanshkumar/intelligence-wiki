Miguel Lázaro-Gredilla is a machine learning researcher specializing in probabilistic graphical models and Bayesian inference. PhD from Universidad Carlos III de Madrid; postdoc at the Gatsby Computational Neuroscience Unit (UCL). Previously at Vicarious AI; now at Google DeepMind.

Co-author of the [[george-2021-clone-structured-cognitive-graphs|CSCG paper]] (George et al. 2021), where his methodological background in variational inference, Gaussian processes, and structured probabilistic models supports the formal apparatus of cloned HMMs (sparse block-emission structure, EM with Dirichlet pseudocount regularization, message-passing inference, online and adaptive EM variants).
