The **motor cortex** is the cortical territory that generates voluntary movement. In this wiki's dynamical-systems framing, it is the clearest case of the foundational closed-loop sensorimotor controller — the primitive from which the rest of the wiki's treatment of intelligence extends.

Following the convention used for [[Mushroom Body]] and [[C. elegans Chemotaxis Circuit]], this page covers the relevant primate subregions together rather than splitting them: **primary motor cortex (M1)**, **dorsal premotor cortex (PMd)**, and **supplementary motor area (SMA)**. Separate pages would be warranted if a source motivated them.

## Subregions

### Primary Motor Cortex (M1)

The output region. Corticospinal projections from M1 drive muscle activity via the spinal cord. Population dynamics during movement are dominated by [[Rotational Dynamics|rotational structure]] — a low-dimensional oscillator that generates multiphasic muscle activity (Churchland et al. 2012). M1 is tangled (nearby states can precede divergent behaviors), whereas SMA is structured for low divergence (Russo et al. 2018, 2019).

### Dorsal Premotor Cortex (PMd)

The preparation region. [[Preparatory Activity|Delay-period activity]] in PMd is tuned to upcoming movement direction, distance, and speed, and sets the initial condition from which M1 movement-period dynamics evolve ([[vyas-2020-computation-through-dynamics|Vyas et al. 2020]] review). PMd implements the **initial-condition hypothesis**: preparatory states select where in state space the movement trajectory will start. Preparation is structured to lie in the [[Null Space Coding|output-null subspace]] so it does not produce movement until a go-cue-triggered state shift transitions the population into movement-generation regions.

### Supplementary Motor Area (SMA)

The context-keeping region. SMA population activity keeps track of context (e.g., elapsed time, trial position in a sequence), and is structured for **low divergence** — trajectories never follow the same path before diverging, so future behavioral outcomes are disambiguable from the current state (Russo et al. 2019). Rotational dynamics are not observed in SMA, distinguishing it from M1 and PMd.

## Core Findings Under the CTD Framework

[[vyas-2020-computation-through-dynamics|Vyas, Golub, Sussillo & Shenoy (2020)]] synthesize the motor-cortex literature under [[Computation Through Dynamics|CTD]]:

1. **Preparatory activity is an initial condition** ([[Preparatory Activity]]). Delay-period PMd activity sets the state from which movement-period dynamics evolve.
2. **Preparation is output-null** ([[Null Space Coding]]). Prep activity lies in the null space of the muscle readout, so it does not drive movement.
3. **Movement is generated by rotational dynamics** ([[Rotational Dynamics]]). A low-dimensional oscillator produces the multiphasic muscle patterns required for reaching and grasping.
4. **The go cue is a condition-invariant signal (CIS)**. A direction-agnostic population-level state shift transitions motor cortex from the preparation region to the movement-generation region (Kaufman 2016).
5. **Motor adaptation operates on preparatory dimensions.** VMR and CF learning shift preparatory states across trials; movement-period dynamics are relatively preserved (Vyas 2018, Perich 2018, Sheahan 2016).

## Manifold-Constrained Learning

BCI experiments in motor cortex are the strongest empirical evidence for manifolds as real constraints on learning. Key results ([[Neural Manifolds]] for full treatment):

- **Sadtler et al. (2014)**: monkeys rapidly learn BCI perturbations that require within-manifold dynamics but struggle with off-manifold perturbations.
- **Golub et al. (2018)**: within-manifold learning happens by **neural reassociation** — animals re-use existing states in new pairings, rather than discovering new states.
- **Oby et al. (2019)**: multi-day training can access out-of-repertoire and off-manifold states, revealing multiple learning mechanisms at different timescales.

Interpretation for this wiki: the intrinsic motor-cortex manifold is the **control-theoretic repertoire** that connectivity and cellular biophysics make accessible. Fast learning works within it; slow learning reshapes it.

## Relation to the Organizing Principle

Motor cortex is the foundational case of the closed-loop sensorimotor control that this wiki treats as the basis of intelligence. The prepare → move → integrate feedback → update loop is implemented here in explicit, quantifiable form:

- **Prepare** — PMd initial condition ([[Preparatory Activity]]).
- **Move** — M1 rotational pattern generation ([[Rotational Dynamics]]).
- **Hide from actuator** — output-null / output-potent decomposition ([[Null Space Coding]]).
- **Maintain context** — SMA low-divergence trajectories.
- **Update** — adaptation shifts preparatory states.

Every more "cognitive" case in the CTD literature — timing, decision-making, working memory, abstract categorization — is treated as the same dynamical machinery extended over longer timescales or more latent variables. Motor cortex is where the machinery is directly visible.
