# Overview

This wiki investigates biological intelligence through a specific lens: **intelligence begins with closed-loop sensorimotor control.** Memory, learning, and cognition are not separate faculties — they are extensions that improve an organism's control over progressively longer timescales, more latent variables, and more counterfactual worlds.

The simplest nervous systems already close the loop: sense, predict, act, update. Everything more complex — synaptic plasticity, memory consolidation, hierarchical representations, planning — can be understood as machinery that extends this basic loop's reach. The wiki traces this progression across levels:

- **The immediate loop** — sensorimotor prediction and control at the timescale of milliseconds to seconds (reflexes, oscillatory dynamics, motor commands)
- **Extending in time** — plasticity mechanisms (LTP/LTD, Hebbian learning) that let past experience modify the loop's parameters, and sleep-dependent consolidation that transfers learned contingencies from fast hippocampal storage to slow cortical models
- **Extending over latent variables** — sparse representations that carve the sensory world into categories the control system can act on, and hierarchical credit assignment that tunes deep processing pipelines to serve downstream action
- **Extending over counterfactuals** — internal models that support planning, imagination, and generalization beyond direct experience

A related hypothesis motivates the evolutionary dimension: genes encode behavior not by specifying circuits, but by shaping neurons' nonlinear feedback control algorithms, producing conserved macroscopic dynamical structures (e.g., limit cycle attractors) with high probability despite unique microscopic configurations. This is the control-theoretic reading of [[Degeneracy]].

## Emerging Themes

### Local rules can produce emergent reward-seeking

[[li-2024-prediction-noise-reward|Li et al. (2024)]] demonstrates that two simple ingredients — local [[Predictive Coding|predictive updates]] and [[Neural Noise|internal noise]] — are sufficient for neural networks to exhibit flexible reward-seeking behavior. Networks autonomously explore and exploit, adapt to environmental changes, and form task preferences, all without an external reward function. The mechanism relies on [[Attractor Dynamics]]: rewarding states are stable attractors under noise, while unrewarding states are unstable.

This has implications for understanding what evolution invested in. If basic reward-seeking is "free" once neurons do local prediction and have intrinsic noise (biophysically unavoidable), then evolution's investment was not in inventing goal-directed behavior but in machinery to **shape and control** it — steering subsystems, neuromodulation, specialized architectures. The role of dopamine may be more subtle than standard RL assumes: not defining what's rewarding, but biasing *which* emergent goals get preferred through learning rate modulation.

Key open question: can this minimal mechanism scale beyond toy environments to handle the challenges real organisms face (delayed rewards, high-dimensional sensory input, aversive stimuli)?

### Oscillatory structure encodes spatial information

[[lubenov-2009-theta-travelling-waves|Lubenov & Siapas (2009)]] showed that [[Theta Oscillations]] in the [[Hippocampus]] are not synchronized — they are **travelling waves** propagating along the septotemporal axis. Combined with [[Phase Precession]] (spike timing shifting with position within a place field), this means the hippocampus instantaneously encodes a **segment** of space, not a point. The wave's wavelength matches the hippocampal length, giving each septotemporal position a unique phase.

This is an example of "the geometry *is* the computation" — the travelling wave isn't a description layered on top of the real computation, but the mechanism by which the hippocampus achieves its topographic spatial mapping. The [[Place Cells|place field]] size gradient along the septotemporal axis (small septal, large temporal) adds a resolution dimension: within each theta cycle, hippocampal output sweeps from fine to coarse spatial resolution.

Key open question: is the travelling wave property **causally necessary** for spatial behavior? The experiment — imposing synchronous theta while preserving frequency and power — has not yet been done.

### The bottleneck is the representation, not the learning rule

[[meister-2022-learning-fast-slow|Meister (2022)]] surveys learning rates across species and tasks, finding a 10,000× range — from one-shot fear conditioning to tens of thousands of trials for laboratory 2AFC tasks. The critical variable is not task complexity but whether the animal already has a [[Sparse Coding|sparse, high-dimensional neural code]] for the relevant events.

[[Hebbian Learning]] is **fast** — synapses can modify in a single trial. But Hebbian plasticity is **local** — each synapse only sees its own pre/post activity. With distributed (non-sparse) codes, this locality causes interference: shared neurons can't distinguish which stimulus caused them to fire. Sparse codes eliminate this problem by ensuring each synapse participates in at most one association.

This converges with [[li-2024-prediction-noise-reward|Li et al. (2024)]] on a broader theme: **the learning rules themselves are simple; evolution's investment was elsewhere.** PaN shows simple prediction + noise gives you reward-seeking for free. Meister shows simple Hebbian plasticity gives you fast associative learning — *if* the right representations exist. Both papers redirect attention from the learning algorithm to the architecture, representations, and control systems that enable it.

Two sources of sparse codes: (1) genetic/evolutionary hardwiring for ecologically critical events, and (2) unsupervised learning from lifetime experience (the cortical hierarchy progressively sparsifies representations). Meister proposes that the Bayesian prior **is** the sparse code — lacking a code for a contingency is what makes it seem implausible.

Key open question: does passive exposure to task stimuli (without reinforcement) build sparse codes that accelerate subsequent learning? This would directly test whether the bottleneck is representational.

### The right level of description is macroscopic dynamics, not individual neurons

[[brennan-2019-conserved-macroscopic-dynamics|Brennan & Proekt (2019)]] demonstrate that even in *C. elegans* — the simplest model nervous system, with a fully mapped connectome and genetically identical animals — individual neuron activation patterns differ consistently across worms performing the same behavior. Averaging neuron activity across individuals destroys behaviorally relevant information.

Yet the **macroscopic dynamics** are conserved: the system traces cyclic loops through a low-dimensional [[Neural Manifolds|manifold]] parameterized by just two variables (phase and loop identity). This manifold, built from ~5% of neurons, predicts behavioral switches up to 30 seconds ahead and generalizes across animals observed years apart.

This is [[Degeneracy]]: many microscopic configurations → same macroscopic function. Evolution selects on behavior, not on individual neuron activation, so there is no pressure for microscopic consistency. The implication reinforces the emerging theme from multiple sources: **the learning rules and computational primitives are simple; the magic is in the collective organization** — whether that's the architecture (Bennett), the representations ([[meister-2022-learning-fast-slow|Meister]]), or the dynamics (Brennan & Proekt).

The [[Attractor Dynamics|attractor]] framework connects across scales: [[li-2024-prediction-noise-reward|Li et al. (2024)]] shows attractors organizing reward-seeking behavior in a toy model; Brennan & Proekt show limit cycle attractors organizing motor behavior in a real nervous system. In both cases, [[Neural Noise|noise]] plays a functional role — destabilizing unrewarding states in PaN, driving behavioral transitions at manifold merge points in *C. elegans*.

[[brennan-2023-looper-computational-scaffold|Brennan et al. (2023)]] push this further with LOOPER, which extracts **computational scaffolds** — graphs of branching and merging 1D trajectories that directly reveal what information a system stores and when it makes decisions. The key demonstration: a monkey PFC and an RNN trained on the same working memory task produce the same computational scaffold despite completely different neuronal activity. But the two systems employ different strategies — the monkey learns the general algorithm, the RNN exploits dataset statistics and fails on specific novel stimuli that the scaffold predicts. This extends [[Degeneracy]] from "same dynamics despite different neuron activations" to "same computational structure despite different architectures entirely." It also suggests biological systems are under selection pressure for generalization in a way artificial networks are not.

### Known biology is sufficient for hierarchical credit assignment

Six papers now form a complete lineage from conceptual insight through computational synthesis to experimental validation of [[Credit Assignment]] in cortical hierarchies, developed across four labs (Richards' group in Toronto/McGill, Senn's group in Bern, Costa's group in Bristol, and Harnett's group at MIT):

[[kording-konig-2001-two-integration-sites|Kording & Konig (2001)]] first recognized that cortical pyramidal neurons' two integration sites — basal and apical dendrites — provide **two independent variables per neuron**, sufficient to implement gradient-based learning rules that single-site point neurons structurally cannot. Their proof: under the right assumptions, burst rate D replaces the backpropagation error δ, activity A flows feedforward via single spikes, and facilitating/depressing synapses multiplex both signals on the same axon. Crucially, they proved a **no-go result** for one-site neurons: whenever the learning objective is not exclusively a function of the neuron's activity, a single-site neuron must distort its activity representation to carry the learning signal — producing a fundamental tradeoff between representing and learning.

[[guerguiev-2017-segregated-dendrites|Guerguiev, Lillicrap & Richards (2017)]] provided the first **spiking** implementation. Three-compartment neurons (soma, basal, apical) alternate between forward and plateau phases; during plateau phases, apical dendrites produce calcium-spike-like plateau potentials encoding feedback information. With random feedback weights and no weight transport, the network achieves 3.28% on MNIST and produces hierarchical representations. The most striking finding: **feedback alignment emerges during training** — weight updates are initially orthogonal to backprop (~65°) but converge toward the backprop gradient (~40°) as the network learns to do credit assignment. This suggests that calibrating feedback pathways (learning how to learn) could be a developmental process. The two-phase requirement is the main biological limitation.

[[sacramento-2018-dendritic-microcircuits-backprop|Sacramento et al. (2018)]] eliminated the phase requirement and **unified [[Predictive Coding]] with credit assignment**. Three-compartment pyramidal neurons are paired with lateral SST interneurons that learn to predict and cancel top-down feedback, establishing a **self-predicting state** where apical dendrites are silent. When a novel teaching signal arrives at the output, interneurons can't explain it away → prediction errors appear in apical dendrites → these errors propagate backward through the network. In the weak-feedback limit, this is analytically identical to backpropagation. The key insight: the prediction error at the apical dendrite *is* the backpropagated gradient — predictive coding and backpropagation are not competing theories but two descriptions of the same dendritic microcircuit.

[[payeur-2020-burst-dependent-credit-assignment|Payeur et al. (2020)]] brought the framework to biophysical maturity, implementing the mechanism with spiking two-compartment neurons, explicit VGCCs and dendritic calcium spikes, inhibitory linearization via SST+ interneurons, and learned feedback alignment. The burst-dependent plasticity rule, combined with short-term plasticity for signal multiplexing and inhibitory microcircuits for linearization, approximates backpropagation gradients at the ensemble level and scales to CIFAR-10.

[[greedy-2022-single-phase-burstccn|Greedy et al. (2022)]] synthesized the lineage with **BurstCCN**, resolving both remaining limitations. The key innovation: a **Q feedback pathway** (STD connections to apical dendrites) that learns to cancel baseline burst activity from the Y pathway (STF connections via SST+ interneurons). This Q-Y cancellation achieves Sacramento's self-predicting state through learned feedback weights rather than a separate interneuron population. The result: single-phase learning (eliminating Guerguiev's and Burstprop's phase requirement), depth scaling to 9 layers (solving Sacramento's EDN degradation), and CIFAR-10 performance (~23%). Rui Ponte Costa — co-author on Sacramento (2018) from Senn's group — is senior author, bridging the Bern and Bristol threads.

[[francioni-2026-vectorized-dendritic-signals|Francioni et al. (2026)]] close the loop from theory to experiment. Using simultaneous two-photon imaging of L5 pyramidal neuron somas and distal apical dendrites during a neurofeedback BCI task, they isolate **somato-dendritic (SD) residuals** — the dendritic calcium signal not predicted by somatic activity. These residuals are vectorized and cell-specific: during error reduction, neurons whose activity drives toward the target (P+) have amplified dendrites while those driving away (P-) have attenuated dendrites. This is exactly the cell-specific teaching signal the computational lineage predicted. Optogenetic activation of **NDNF+ layer 1 interneurons** abolishes the vectorized signal and impairs learning — causal evidence that local dendritic computation is necessary for credit assignment. One nuance: SD residuals encode error *derivatives* rather than error magnitudes, slightly favoring target propagation over classical backpropagation. NDNF+ interneurons are a new cell type in the story — the computational models emphasized SST+ Martinotti cells, but Francioni shows that L1 NDNF+ cells gating distal tuft input are equally critical, suggesting a laminar division of labor.

This resolves a tension raised by earlier sources. [[meister-2022-learning-fast-slow|Meister (2022)]] emphasized that [[Hebbian Learning]] is simple and local — the bottleneck is the representation, not the learning rule. But if Hebbian learning is only local, how does the brain coordinate changes across multiple layers to build the hierarchical representations in the first place? The lineage answers: the plasticity rules **are** Hebbian (local dendritic prediction errors × presynaptic rate), but the "post" signal is enriched by dendritic computation to carry credit information from higher areas. The rule is simple and local at the synapse; the architecture (dendritic compartments, interneuron predictions, STP, inhibition) embeds the global structure needed for hierarchical learning.

This also connects to the broader "simple rules, complex architecture" theme: the learning rule itself is a local coincidence detector; the computational sophistication lies in the biological structures — [[Dendritic Computation|dendritic compartmentalization]], differential [[Synapses|short-term plasticity]], and [[Inhibitory Circuits|inhibitory microcircuits]] — that route the right signals to the right compartments. Sacramento adds a specific prediction to this picture: SST interneurons don't just linearize (Payeur) — they actively **learn to predict** top-down feedback, and the failure of that prediction is the error signal. The self-predicting state as network equilibrium connects naturally to the control thesis: the system maintains a homeostatic equilibrium where all predictions are satisfied, and deviations from equilibrium drive corrective learning updates.

### Single neurons are far more powerful than point models suggest

[[jones-2020-dendritic-computation-power|Jones & Kording (2020)]] demonstrate that modeling a single neuron's dendritic tree as a sparse binary tree ANN — where each branch imposes a nonlinearity before summation at the soma — yields a computational unit that significantly outperforms linear classifiers and approaches the performance of a parameter-matched 2-layer fully connected network on standard benchmarks (MNIST, CIFAR-10). The key additional finding: **repeated synaptic inputs** across dendritic branches (multi-synaptic boutons, or MSBs) monotonically increase performance, and LTP induces a 6-fold increase in MSBs — meaning learning doesn't just tune weights but **grows the internal architecture** of individual neurons.

This deepens the "simple rules, complex architecture" theme in two ways. First, the "architecture" that matters isn't just the network graph — it includes the internal structure of each node. A network of dendritic neurons is far more powerful than a network of point neurons with the same connectivity. Second, the architecture is not static: [[Hebbian Learning|Hebbian plasticity]] itself reshapes the computational substrate via MSB formation, blurring the line between "learning the weights" and "learning the architecture." Combined with Payeur's insight that dendrites also enable [[Credit Assignment|hierarchical credit assignment]], dendritic computation emerges as a central theme — the single neuron is not a simple processing element but a sophisticated computational module that is simultaneously being tuned by learning and reshaped by it.

### Category representations are gradually built, not instantaneously assigned

[[reinert-2021-pfc-categorization|Reinert et al. (2021)]] provide a direct window into how the brain builds new representations during learning. Using chronic two-photon imaging of mouse [[Prefrontal Cortex|mPFC]] across weeks of visual categorization training, they show that category-selective neurons emerge gradually from near-zero baseline — representations are built up as part of semantic memory, not recruited ad hoc. Once built, these representations support immediate generalization to novel stimuli.

The rule-switch dynamics reveal unexpected complexity: when the categorization rule changes, Go-preferring neurons remap to the new category while NoGo-preferring neurons are replaced by a new population entirely. This asymmetry suggests that even within a single brain region, different subpopulations use different update mechanisms — some representations are flexible, others are rebuilt from scratch.

This connects to the representation-building theme from [[meister-2022-learning-fast-slow|Meister (2022)]]: the slow timescale of learning in lab categorization tasks is consistent with the gradual construction of representations. However, Reinert images PFC (the decision/readout level), not sensory cortex (where Meister's sparse codes would be built). Whether the PFC bottleneck reflects slow upstream sensory code construction or slow rule learning in PFC itself remains an important open question — one that would require simultaneous imaging of sensory and prefrontal areas to resolve.

### Natural tasks reveal learning that trial-based paradigms hide

[[rosenberg-2021-labyrinth-learning|Rosenberg et al. (2021)]] put mice into a 6-level binary-tree labyrinth overnight, unsupervised, with no trial structure. The result is the empirical complement to [[meister-2022-learning-fast-slow|Meister (2022)]]: in the same lab, same species, an ethologically natural task produces ~20× more decisions per hour and exposes behavioral phenomena invisible in 2AFC — few-shot reward learning (~10 experiences), **one-shot** home-path learning, discontinuous **sudden-insight** transitions ([[Sudden Insight]]), and persistent exploration even in unrewarded animals ([[intrinsic-exploration|intrinsic exploration]]). Together these papers form the wiki's [[ethological-paradigms|ethological paradigms]] thread: trial-based tasks are measuring representation-building, not learning.

The striking finding for the control thesis is the **cross-animal consistency of exploration biases**. Four local turning parameters at T-junctions (streak/switch, forward/away, etc.) capture most of the gap between mouse and random-walk efficiency, and those parameters are strikingly stable across 19 mice (SD ~0.03). This is behavioral-level [[Degeneracy]]: presumably variable microscopic implementations, conserved macroscopic structure (the four-parameter decision rule). It is precisely what a genes→control→structure hypothesis predicts — genes constrain local decision rules at each T-junction, and efficient global exploration emerges as a high-probability consequence without any gene needing to specify "visit all 64 nodes." The paper does not prove the hypothesis, but it is the cleanest mammalian-behavioral touchpoint for it in the wiki so far.

### Cognitive navigation is ancient chemotaxis extended by an internal signal generator

[[zhang-2024-endotaxis-neuromorphic-navigation|Zhang et al. (2024)]] propose **[[Endotaxis]]**: a simple 3-layer Hebbian circuit (point cells → recurrent map cells → goal cells) that produces an internal "virtual odor" — a signal provably monotonic in graph distance to a chosen target. Gradient ascent on this signal ≡ shortest-path navigation. The key move is that the circuit does not invent new machinery for cognitive navigation; it **feeds an internally generated gradient signal to the ancient chemotaxis controller** that every motile animal already has. Map cells learn the environment's adjacency matrix by Hebbian coincidence; goal cells imprint target locations in one shot; a mode switch selects which virtual odor gets routed to the taxis module.

This is the sharpest circuit-level instantiation in the wiki of the organizing principle that intelligence **extends** sensorimotor control rather than replacing it. The hard evolutionary work (chemotaxis) was done in bacteria. Cognitive navigation required adding only an internal signal-generation stage in front of the pre-existing controller. The same "sparse place-like code → convergent Hebbian readout → neuromodulated plasticity" motif is realized in the insect [[Mushroom Body]] (Kenyon cells → MBONs) and proposed for the vertebrate [[Hippocampus]] — the same computational solution independently reached by deeply divergent lineages.

Endotaxis also tightly couples the [[rosenberg-2021-labyrinth-learning|Rosenberg et al. (2021)]] behavioral observations to circuit mechanism: one-shot homing falls out of a single goal synapse being potentiated at the entrance before the map exists; few-shot reward learning falls out of one-shot goal-synapse imprinting after the map is learned; efficient patrolling falls out of adding habituation to point cells. Three behaviors, one circuit, local rules throughout. The mathematics — `Y = (γ⁻¹I − A)⁻¹` whose matrix elements are monotonic in graph distance — is formally similar to the [[Successor Representation]] but with a different interpretation (neural gain vs. temporal discount), suggesting a shared representational primitive readable either as value function or as goal gradient.

### Sleep provides the hippocampal-cortical bridge for memory consolidation

[[wierzynski-2009-sleep-hpc-pfc|Wierzynski et al. (2009)]] demonstrate that during slow-wave sleep, [[Hippocampus|hippocampal]] CA1 cells fire 0-100 ms before [[Prefrontal Cortex|mPFC]] cells, with this directional timing driven by sharp-wave/ripple (SWR) events and falling within the window for [[Long-Term Potentiation|spike-timing-dependent plasticity]]. This coupling is nearly abolished during REM sleep, suggesting a two-stage consolidation model: SWS for hippocampus-to-cortex transfer, REM for internal cortical reorganization.

This connects Reinert's observation of gradually emerging PFC representations to a possible mechanism: SWR-driven hippocampal reactivation during sleep could progressively build and refine cortical category representations over many sleep cycles. The weeks-long timescale of PFC representation building (Reinert) is consistent with consolidation requiring repeated SWR-driven reinforcement. It also extends the Siapas lab's work across behavioral states — [[lubenov-2009-theta-travelling-waves|Lubenov & Siapas (2009)]] showed theta travelling waves structure hippocampal computation during waking, while Wierzynski et al. show SWR-driven coupling structures hippocampal-cortical communication during sleep. Same circuit, different oscillatory modes, different computational needs.

### The circuit ↔ behavior mapping is many-to-many in both directions

[[banerjee-2023-life-stage-chemosensation|Banerjee et al. (2023)]] establish the complement to classical [[Degeneracy]]. In *C. elegans* CO₂ chemotaxis, starved adults and dauer larvae share the same genome, the same anatomical connectome, and the same behavioral valence (attraction) — yet different interneurons are recruited with different polarities, producing different motor programs. **AIB** is robustly excited by CO₂ in dauers and silent in adults; **AVE** is inhibited in dauers and silent in adults. The reconfiguration is gated by state-dependent BAG–AIB gap junction activity (via the CHE-7 innexin) and by daf-2 insulin/IGF signaling. Ablating AIB in adults partially dauer-izes their behavior, but artificially raising AIB excitability does not — AIB is necessary but not sufficient; multiple circuit components combine.

[[brennan-2019-conserved-macroscopic-dynamics|Brennan & Proekt (2019)]] showed *different implementations → same behavior* across individuals. Banerjee shows the complementary direction: *same implementation, different behaviors* across states of one individual. Together they establish that the mapping between neural circuit and behavior is many-to-many in both directions — a framing now captured by [[Circuit-Behavior Mapping]] as the umbrella, with [[Context-Dependent Circuit Reconfiguration]] as the specific complement to degeneracy. The anatomical connectome underdetermines the functional circuit; state — here a systemic endocrine signal acting on electrical synapse composition — selects among effective circuits over the same anatomy.

This has two consequences for the control thesis. First, it reinforces that control-level descriptors (valence, policy, attractor structure) are what is conserved or reconfigured; the circuit is a substrate that flexibly implements them. Second, it enriches the endotaxis framing ([[zhang-2024-endotaxis-neuromorphic-navigation|Zhang et al., 2024]]): the ancient chemotaxis controller that cognitive navigation repurposes is itself not a fixed primitive — even in *C. elegans*, it is state-reconfigurable. Flexibility is present from the foundation upward.

### Computation Through Dynamics names the stance the wiki has been taking

[[vyas-2020-computation-through-dynamics|Vyas, Golub, Sussillo & Shenoy (2020)]] codify as [[Computation Through Dynamics|CTD]] the framework that has been implicit across many of the sources already ingested — [[brennan-2019-conserved-macroscopic-dynamics|Brennan & Proekt (2019)]]'s manifolds, [[brennan-2023-looper-computational-scaffold|Brennan et al. (2023)]]'s computational scaffolds, [[li-2024-prediction-noise-reward|Li et al. (2024)]]'s attractor-driven PaN. The core claim: a neural population's job is to *be* a dynamical system `dx/dt = f(x, u)` whose temporal evolution *is* the computation, rather than a code whose instantaneous values represent the answer. Initial conditions are computed objects; slow inputs are contextual modulators that reshape `f` locally; the analysis tool of choice is fixed-point finding and linearization in trained RNNs used as hypothesis generators.

For the control thesis, the Vyas review is the most direct formalization available. Motor cortex is the foundational closed-loop controller — prepare, move, sense feedback, update — and CTD supplies the formal language for each stage: [[Preparatory Activity|preparatory activity as initial condition]] (PMd sets the state from which movement evolves), [[Null Space Coding|output-null preparation]] (the computation hides from the muscles via a linear-algebraic trick), [[Rotational Dynamics|rotational pattern generation]] (M1 is an oscillator that produces muscle activity rather than representing it), and context-as-slow-input (for timing, decision, working memory extensions). Every more "cognitive" case in the CTD literature — timing, line-attractor decisions ([[brennan-2023-looper-computational-scaffold|Mante 2013 and the same lineage Brennan 2023 operates in]]), working memory, Bayesian priors as curved manifolds — is treated as the same machinery extended over longer timescales and more latent variables. That is also the wiki's organizing principle.

The most consequential empirical result for the control thesis in this review is **manifold-constrained learning** (Sadtler 2014, Golub 2018, Oby 2019). In primate BCI experiments, within-manifold perturbations are learned in hours by **neural reassociation** — re-using existing states in new pairings, not discovering new states. Off-manifold perturbations require substantial synaptic-weight change and take days. The intrinsic [[Neural Manifolds|manifold]] is the dynamical repertoire connectivity and biophysics make accessible; fast learning operates inside it, slow structural change reshapes it. This is exactly what the control-theoretic reading of [[Degeneracy]] predicts at the learning level, and it operationalizes [[Memory Consolidation|consolidation]] as the mechanism for the slower, rarer reshaping.

### A random recurrent network has a dynamical substrate with two phases

[[sompolinsky-1988-chaos-random-networks|Sompolinsky, Crisanti & Sommers (1988)]] — henceforth SCS — establish what recurrent networks *generically* do before training shapes them. For a rate network with random asymmetric Gaussian couplings (variance J²/N), the only intensive parameter is the gain `gJ`. In the thermodynamic limit (N → ∞), there are exactly two phases: a silent fixed point for gJ < 1, and **deterministic high-dimensional chaos** for gJ > 1. Fixed points, limit cycles, and static "spin-glass" states all exist as candidate solutions above the transition, but all are unstable to fluctuations — chaos is the stable attractor. The result is proved via [[Dynamical Mean-Field Theory|DMFT]], which reduces the N-body dynamics to a single self-consistent stochastic neuron.

This matters for the wiki in three ways.

First, **SCS is the substrate under [[Computation Through Dynamics|CTD]]**. Trained RNNs analyzed by Sussillo, Mante, Sohn and others are typically initialized with gJ slightly above 1 — in the chaotic regime. The [[Rotational Dynamics|rotations]], [[Preparatory Activity|preparatory initial conditions]], line-attractor decisions, and [[Null Space Coding|null-space]] structures that training carves out are attractor structures *pulled from* the chaotic reservoir. The Sadtler/Golub/Oby BCI result — that fast learning reassociates within the intrinsic [[Neural Manifolds|manifold]] while off-manifold learning requires structural change — takes on a sharper reading: the intrinsic manifold is the SCS-style chaotic flow that connectivity has shaped, and training further carves task-relevant subspaces out of it.

Second, SCS is the theoretical anchor of **[[Edge of Chaos]]**. Near gJ = 1, correlation times diverge (τ ∝ 1/ε), the Lyapunov exponent vanishes as ε²/2, and the flow becomes marginally chaotic — long memory, high input sensitivity. This is the theoretical basis for reservoir computing and for the common practice of initializing trainable RNNs at the edge. For the control thesis: if cortex operates near criticality (as balanced E-I networks and neuronal avalanche results suggest), the edge provides a flexible dynamical repertoire that genes-shaped connectivity and experience-driven plasticity can then sculpt into specific control policies.

Third, SCS is the canonical model of **deterministic chaos as a source of apparent [[Neural Noise|neural variability]]**. The chaos here is not stochastic — the couplings are fixed and the dynamics are deterministic — yet the activity is unpredictable at long times and looks statistically like noise. This is a concrete alternative (or complement) to stochastic ion-channel noise as the source of cortical trial-to-trial variability. It also closes a conceptual loop: [[li-2024-prediction-noise-reward|Li et al. (2024)]]'s PaN uses noise to destabilize unrewarding attractor states, and Brennan & Proekt's manifold picture invokes noise at merge points — SCS shows that much of that "noise" can be generated by the recurrent dynamics themselves, without appeal to microscopic stochasticity.

A central open question SCS poses for the rest of the wiki: what changes when couplings are **structured** rather than i.i.d.? Dale's law, sparsity, low-rank task-relevant perturbations (Mastrogiuseppe & Ostojic 2018; Schuessler 2020), and spatial connectivity all shift or restructure the transition. If the brain's connectivity can be decomposed as "random background + low-rank task-relevant component," the chaotic substrate is generic and the task structure is minimal-footprint — which resonates with the recurring wiki theme that simple rules plus complex architecture suffice.

### Sparsely connected E-I spiking networks have four regimes tunable by balance and drive

[[brunel-1999-sparsely-connected-networks|Brunel (2000)]] is the spiking-network sibling of SCS. It extends the random-network analysis from rate units to sparsely connected populations of excitatory and inhibitory integrate-and-fire neurons, with explicit Dale's law, and solves the self-consistent single-neuron problem via a Fokker-Planck equation for the membrane-potential distribution rather than a DMFT autocorrelation equation. The same intellectual move — reducing an N-body random-network problem to a one-neuron problem closed self-consistently by the ensemble statistics — produces a much richer phase diagram: four generic regimes organized by just two parameters, the relative inhibition strength `g = JI/JE` and the external drive `ν_ext/ν_thr`.

- **AI (asynchronous irregular)**: low firing rates, CV ≈ 1, weak pairwise correlations, stationary global activity. This is the regime that matches recorded spontaneous cortical activity. It requires `g > 4` — inhibition-dominated balance — and is generated entirely by the **quenched random connectivity**, not by stochastic external noise (σ_ext → 0 leaves CV ≈ 1 unchanged).
- **SR / AR / SI**: the other three regimes — synchronous-regular lockstep firing, asynchronous-regular clock-like firing, and synchronous-irregular population oscillations — occupy the excitation-dominated, weak-inhibition, and strong-drive corners of the same phase diagram. They are separated by **Hopf bifurcations** from the AI state, and by a saddle-node bifurcation from low-activity regimes.

This has three consequences for the wiki.

First, **the AI state is the spiking-level substrate under [[Computation Through Dynamics|CTD]]**. Where SCS gives the rate-level chaotic reservoir, Brunel gives the spiking-level irregular reservoir. Cortical circuits that CTD-style analyses (Mante, Sussillo, Sohn, Vyas) treat as trainable substrates appear to sit in the AI regime — balanced, irregular, and input-sensitive. The trained manifolds, rotations, and line attractors identified by CTD are low-rank structure carved out of the AI background. [[Excitation-Inhibition Balance]] is the concrete biological condition that puts the system there; [[Asynchronous Irregular State]] is what that condition generically produces.

Second, **gamma (~20–60 Hz) and sharp-wave ripples (~100–200 Hz) are the same network** in two different regimes of the same (g, ν_ext) plane. Gamma arises at the AI ↔ SI boundary at a frequency set by membrane and synaptic time constants; ripples arise at the SR ↔ SI boundary at `f ≈ 1/2D` set by the synaptic delay D. One balanced network, two Hopf bifurcations, two oscillation families. This is a strong argument against viewing gamma and ripple as products of distinct circuits — they are selectable regimes of a shared substrate. It also gives a concrete mechanistic handle on what [[Theta Oscillations|theta]] and ripple events are doing during [[wierzynski-2009-sleep-hpc-pfc|SWS consolidation]]: selecting, via neuromodulatory tuning of balance, which dynamical regime the hippocampal-cortical loop occupies.

Third, **finite-size cortex sits near criticality**. The AI state has long-lived damped oscillations in its global activity autocorrelation, with coherence time scaling as `1/ε` where `ε ≡ C/N` is the connection sparsity. In the N → ∞ limit the AI state becomes truly stationary; at finite N, critical-slowing-style residual coherence persists. The balance point g = 4 is the spiking analogue of SCS's gJ = 1 — the [[Edge of Chaos|edge-of-chaos]] framing carries directly from rate to spiking, with `(g, ν_ext)` replacing the single rate-network gain. This gives a concrete operating-point question for cortex: is g actively regulated to a specific setpoint by inhibitory plasticity and homeostatic mechanisms, and if so, to what value?

Brunel also reshapes the neural-noise picture. SCS identified deterministic chaos as an intrinsic source of apparent variability at the rate level; Brunel identifies a second, spiking-level mechanism — the quenched random connectivity itself produces high-variance, noise-like single-cell statistics with no stochastic channel noise required. Two distinct routes to "noise-without-stochasticity," both generic in random-network ensembles, both providing the variability that [[li-2024-prediction-noise-reward|PaN]]-style attractor destabilization and Brennan-style manifold-transition dynamics depend on.

### Structured rate-level E-I dynamics: Wilson-Cowan as the mesoscopic mapping

[[wilson-cowan-1972-ei-populations|Wilson & Cowan (1972)]] is the **structured complement** to the random-network thread (SCS at the rate level, Brunel at the spiking level). Rather than asking what a random asymmetric recurrent network does, WC asks what a *structured* E-I population does — one where the cell-type dichotomy and sigmoid response nonlinearity are imposed by construction, matching the structural constraints of real cortex. The answer is strikingly tractable: a two-variable mean-field model (E, I) has exactly **three generic dynamical regimes** in its phase plane, selected by inequalities on just four coupling strengths (c₁, c₂, c₃, c₄) and two sigmoid slopes (aₑ, aᵢ):

- **Simple [[Hysteresis|hysteresis]]** when `c₁ > 9/aₑ` — two stable fixed points separated by a saddle; the upper state is the persistent-activity substrate Hebb (1949) proposed for short-term memory.
- **Multiple hysteresis** when `aₑaᵢc₂c₃ > (aₑc₁ − 9)(aᵢc₄ + 9)` — up to five fixed points, three stable; uniquely enabled by E-I negative feedback and impossible in purely excitatory populations.
- **[[Limit Cycle|Limit-cycle attractor]]** when `c₁aₑ > c₄aᵢ + 18` — E-E coupling must exceed I-I coupling by a margin; frequency and amplitude both grow monotonically with input intensity, realizing a frequency-coded intensity representation.

Wilson-Cowan's **Theorem 4** is the formal ancestor of everything the wiki has been saying about substrate flexibility: *any E-I population capable of a limit cycle under some bias must also exhibit simple hysteresis under some other bias*. A single genetically-specified substrate can express multiple dynamical regimes — rhythm and memory, pattern-generation and persistent-activity — as neuromodulatory or attentional inputs shift it across parameter-space boundaries. This is the rate-level expression of the [[Context-Dependent Circuit Reconfiguration]] and [[Circuit-Behavior Mapping]] theses already developed at the circuit level in *C. elegans* ([[banerjee-2023-life-stage-chemosensation|Banerjee 2023]]).

Three consequences for the wiki.

First, WC **closes the gap in the control-theoretic reading of [[Degeneracy]]**. The genes→control→structure hypothesis says that genes constrain neurons' local nonlinear feedback control algorithms such that certain macroscopic dynamical structures (limit cycles for locomotion, bistability for working memory) emerge with high probability. WC makes this mapping explicit: gene expression sets the aggregate coupling strengths c₁...c₄ and sigmoid slopes aₑ, aᵢ; the dynamical repertoire follows directly from inequalities on those population-level parameters; the microscopic configuration that realizes them is free to vary. The conserved limit-cycle attractors in *C. elegans* locomotion ([[brennan-2019-conserved-macroscopic-dynamics|Brennan & Proekt 2019]]) and the conserved four-parameter turning rule in mouse labyrinth exploration ([[rosenberg-2021-labyrinth-learning|Rosenberg et al. 2021]]) both fit this template: evolution selects on the macroscopic attractor structure; the mesoscopic coupling parameters are what genes tune; the microscopic wiring is degenerate.

Second, WC is the **rate-level ancestor of [[brunel-1999-sparsely-connected-networks|Brunel (2000)]]'s Hopf bifurcations**. The limit-cycle condition `c₁aₑ > c₄aᵢ + 18` (E-E coupling dominates I-I coupling) and the limit-cycle-from-balanced-state transitions in Brunel's spiking phase diagram (AI → SI as the network crosses into a Hopf bifurcation) are the same dynamical phenomenon — periodic orbits born from the loss of fixed-point stability when excitation outruns inhibition — expressed at rate vs. spiking levels. Gamma and sharp-wave ripples at the spiking level, cortical rhythms in the Wilson-Cowan 1973 neural field and downstream neural-mass models (Jansen-Rit, TVB), are instances of the same limit-cycle motif. [[Rotational Dynamics|Motor-cortex rotations]] are the same dynamical object at a different scale — low-dimensional oscillators that generate time-structured motor output.

Third, WC is the **founding [[Neural Mass Model]]** and the methodological ancestor of the neural-mass-modeling lineage (Freeman, Jansen-Rit, Dynamic Causal Modeling, The Virtual Brain, Buice-Cowan stochastic Wilson-Cowan). The move — replace N coupled neurons with a small number of population-activity variables; treat the large N and the random local connectivity as justification for mean-field reduction — is the rate-level analog of the DMFT reduction in SCS and the Fokker-Planck reduction in Brunel. Across all three, the intellectual structure is the same: an N-body random-network problem is closed self-consistently at the level of population statistics. WC is the historically earliest instance and the one that makes regime-selection most transparent.

### Perception as inversion of a generative model — the Helmholtz-machine lineage

[[dayan-hinton-1994-helmholtz-machine|Dayan, Hinton, Neal & Zemel (1994)]] crystallize the [[Analysis-by-Synthesis|analysis-by-synthesis]] view — perception is inference of the hidden causes of sensation — into a concrete, trainable connectionist algorithm. The [[Helmholtz Machine]] has two networks with independent weights: a top-down **generative model** θ (what sensations would this cause produce?) and a bottom-up **recognition model** φ (what causes would explain this sensation?). Joint training maximizes a variational lower bound on log-likelihood — the **negative [[Variational Free Energy|Helmholtz free energy]]** `−F(θ, φ) = log p(d|θ) − KL[Q ‖ P]` — which simultaneously fits the generative model and drives the recognition distribution toward the true posterior.

The paper introduces two algorithms. The deterministic version uses mean-field approximations and conjugate-gradient descent. The stochastic version — the [[Wake-Sleep Algorithm]] — uses a purely local delta rule in two phases: **wake** updates generative weights against recognition-sampled targets, **sleep** updates recognition weights against generative-hallucinated targets. No weight transport, no non-local error signals, no MCMC at recognition time.

This matters for the wiki in four ways.

First, it is the **formal root of [[Predictive Coding]] and [[Active Inference]]**. The Helmholtz free energy is the ELBO; [[rao-ballard-1999-predictive-coding|Rao & Ballard's (1999)]] hierarchical predictive coding is a continuous-latent specialization (see next section); Friston's Free Energy Principle generalizes the framework to include action as a third variable agents can use to reduce F. The control-theoretic reading of the brain as an inference engine acting to maintain its predictive model — the thread that runs through most of the wiki's theory pages — is a direct descendant of the variational framing Dayan et al. introduced.

Second, wake-sleep is a **parallel lineage to dendritic credit assignment** ([[kording-konig-2001-two-integration-sites|Kording 2001]] → [[guerguiev-2017-segregated-dendrites|Guerguiev 2017]] → [[sacramento-2018-dendritic-microcircuits-backprop|Sacramento 2018]] → [[payeur-2020-burst-dependent-credit-assignment|Payeur 2020]] → [[greedy-2022-single-phase-burstccn|Greedy 2022]] → [[francioni-2026-vectorized-dendritic-signals|Francioni 2026]]). Both achieve hierarchical learning without weight transport, but partition the problem differently: wake-sleep separates forward and inverse computations in *time* (wake vs. sleep phases, two networks with independent weights), the dendritic lineage separates them in *space* (basal vs. apical compartments, one network with compartmentalized neurons). The two are not mutually exclusive — the dendritic mechanism is a natural candidate for online waking learning, wake-sleep for offline consolidation — and cortex plausibly implements both. Whether they are formally equivalent under some mapping, or occupy genuinely distinct algorithmic niches, is an open question.

Third, wake-sleep gives a **normative role for REM sleep** that complements the SWR-driven SWS consolidation story ([[wierzynski-2009-sleep-hpc-pfc|Wierzynski et al. 2009]]). SWS looks like the wake phase run offline (data-driven replay of real hippocampal traces, tuning cortical generative models); REM looks like the sleep phase (generative hallucination, tuning cortical recognition pathways). The two-stage sleep architecture has the right shape for a two-phase variational algorithm, though the empirical mapping remains loose.

Fourth, the Helmholtz machine is the **perceptual half of closed-loop control**. To act on a world whose causes are hidden — object identity, other agents' intentions, task context — an organism must invert a generative model of sensation. The Helmholtz machine supplies the computational form of that inversion (amortized approximate posterior inference via a bottom-up recognition network) at a cost in biological plausibility (feed-forward recognition misses the evident iterative and lateral components of cortical processing) and in expressive power (factorial Q misses posterior correlations). These are the open frontiers the framework has been pushing on for three decades.

### Hierarchical predictive coding for cortex: the Rao-Ballard model

[[rao-ballard-1999-predictive-coding|Rao & Ballard (1999)]] turn the Helmholtz-machine framework into a concrete cortical model. Where Dayan-Hinton 1994 used discrete binary latents and offline wake-sleep training, Rao-Ballard use **continuous Gaussian latents** with online gradient-descent dynamics, applied directly to natural-image input. The architecture is the now-canonical predictive-coding picture: feedback connections from a higher cortical area carry top-down predictions of activity at the lower area; feedforward connections carry the residual prediction errors. Each level maintains an estimate `r` of its input, the higher level supplies `r^td = f(U^h r^h)` as the prediction, and the residual `(r − r^td)` is propagated upward. The optimization objective `E = (1/σ²)‖I − f(Ur)‖² + (1/σ²_td)‖r − r^td‖² + g(r) + h(U)` is a Gaussian-specialized [[variational-free-energy|variational free energy]] with explicit precision-weighting `(1/σ², 1/σ²_td)` of the prediction errors at each level — the precision concept that Friston later recasts as attentional modulation in [[active-inference|active inference]].

Three results give the framework its empirical traction.

First, the model **trained on natural images recovers V1 simple-cell receptive fields**. With a Gaussian prior on activities, level-1 basis vectors after training resemble localized oriented Gabor / DoG filters — the Hubel-Wiesel canonical signature. With a kurtotic prior `g(r) = α Σ log(1 + r_i²)`, the wavelet-like RFs of [[sparse-coding|Olshausen-Field 1996]] are recovered exactly inside the hierarchical predictive-coding framework. **Sparse coding is what predictive coding does when the prior on activities is heavy-tailed**; the two are not competing accounts of V1 but complementary views of the same hierarchical inference problem.

Second, **endstopping and other extra-classical RF effects emerge as residual-error signatures**. The level-1 error-detecting neurons (those carrying `(r − r^td)` to level 2) show endstopping: strong response to a short bar in their RF, suppression when the bar extends into the surround. The mechanism is a quantitative claim about [[natural-image-statistics|natural-image statistics]]: in natural images, oriented structures tend to extend over `±50` pixels along their dominant orientation (autocorrelation analysis in Fig. 4), so a long bar can be predicted from the surround and the residual vanishes. The same mechanism reproduces orientation-contrast suppression and facilitation, pop-out (a single odd-orientation bar in a homogeneous field), and tonic-phase contextual modulation. Effects previously catalogued as separate computational specializations — curvature detection, line-end detection, illusory-contour computation, perceptual grouping — are unified as the natural behavior of error-detecting neurons in a network optimized for natural-image statistics.

Third, **disabling top-down feedback eliminates extra-classical effects in the model** (82% reduction in endstopping with feedback removed), matching cat-V1 cooling experiments (Bolz & Gilbert 1986; Sandell & Schiller 1982) and the Hupé et al. (1998) figure-ground result. The model gives a specific cell-type prediction: layer 2/3 pyramidal neurons (the source of feedforward axons) carry the residual prediction error. This is the most testable claim of the paper, and a substantial subsequent literature (Keller-Mrsic-Flogel 2018; Attinger 2017; Zmarz & Keller 2016) has begun to provide direct evidence for prediction-error-like signals in mouse V1 superficial layers.

For the wiki this paper closes a load-bearing arch. Several earlier-ingested sources reference Rao-Ballard 1999 as the canonical predictive-coding citation: [[li-2024-prediction-noise-reward|Li et al. (2024)]], [[sacramento-2018-dendritic-microcircuits-backprop|Sacramento et al. (2018)]], the [[helmholtz-machine|Helmholtz Machine]] page, and the [[active-inference|active-inference]] page all cite it. Ingesting the primary source (a) consolidates the predictive-coding lineage onto a single foundational paper; (b) makes explicit the [[hebbian-learning|Hebbian residual-error learning rule]] `dU/dt ∝ (I − f(Ur)) r^T` whose `(error)(activity)^T` form is the lowest common denominator across the unsupervised-learning lineages of theoretical neuroscience (Sacramento dendritic predictive plasticity, wake-sleep delta rule, sparse coding); and (c) supplies the **perceptual subsystem** that the wiki's [[overview|control-theoretic]] organizing principle has been holding a slot for. The hierarchical generative model the cortex learns from natural images *is* the internal world-model on which downstream control policies operate; prediction-error signals propagating up the hierarchy are the sensory-novelty signals that drive both perceptual updating and (under Friston's active-inference extension) action selection. The hierarchy of progressively larger spatial-and-temporal scales of prediction is precisely the wiki's organizing claim that learning extends control over more latent variables and longer counterfactual horizons.

The Rao-Ballard model also pairs naturally with several other threads on the wiki. It is the **inferential / equilibrium counterpart of [[computation-through-dynamics|CTD]]**: where CTD treats motor and decision cortices as dynamical systems whose temporal evolution *is* the computation, Rao-Ballard treats sensory cortex as an equilibrium-finding inference machine whose stable estimate `r` *is* the perceptual content. Sensory cortex is predominantly inferential, motor cortex predominantly dynamical; the two views compose. It is also the **continuous-latent sibling of [[hopfield-1982-collective-computational-abilities|Hopfield (1982)]]**: both descend an energy / negative-log-posterior objective via gradient descent on activities; they differ in the architectures (hierarchical inference vs. fully-connected associative memory) and the priors (continuous Gaussian latents with task structure vs. binary stored attractor patterns), but the energy-minimization frame is shared.

### Trained dynamics can be constructively carved from the chaotic reservoir

[[sussillo-abbott-2009-force-learning|Sussillo & Abbott (2009)]] supply the constructive dual of the random-network substrate story. Where [[sompolinsky-1988-chaos-random-networks|SCS]] proves that a random recurrent network with `gJ > 1` is generically chaotic, and [[brunel-1999-sparsely-connected-networks|Brunel]] and [[wilson-cowan-1972-ei-populations|Wilson-Cowan]] extend that substrate picture to spiking and structured populations, FORCE shows how to **carve task-structured dynamics directly out of the chaotic reservoir** — with the feedback loop kept intact throughout training. The core move is not gradient descent reducing large errors; it is an aggressive online learning rule (recursive least squares with per-PC effective rates) that keeps the error small from the very first time step, so the network never sees itself failing — it only sees itself succeeding along a trajectory that training progressively tightens. Chaos is not an obstacle: the best performance is near `g ≈ 1.5`, and training fails above `g ≈ 1.56`, giving an *operational* definition of [[Edge of Chaos|edge of chaos]] — the largest gain at which FORCE can still suppress chaos into a coherent orbit. Below the SCS transition, the network lacks the rich spontaneous dynamics that training needs as raw material.

Three consequences for the wiki.

First, FORCE is the **constructive existence proof underlying [[Computation Through Dynamics|CTD]]**. Vyas et al.'s review takes trained RNNs as hypothesis generators for biological dynamics, but it presumes that arbitrary task-relevant dynamics can in fact be trained into a recurrent substrate. FORCE is where that presumption was first established, and it does so with an explicit biological reading: the network is kept closed-loop throughout training (no open-loop teacher-forcing), and learning is single-shot at the synapse level rather than offline backpropagation through time. PCA of trained activity reveals a consistent low-dimensional structure — roughly 8 output-relevant principal components, with the top ~50 uniquely determined across training runs — which is the *in silico* counterpart of the ~10D empirical [[Neural Manifolds|manifolds]] observed in motor cortex. Manifold-constrained BCI learning (Sadtler 2014 et seq.) and FORCE trained manifolds are two views of the same object: low-rank task-relevant structure carved out of a high-dimensional dynamical reservoir.

Second, FORCE gives a **concrete division-of-labor hypothesis for motor cortex + cerebellum/basal ganglia**. The paper's three architectures — readout-feedback (1A), separate-feedback-network (1B), and within-generator internal modifications (1C) — map onto distinct biological scenarios. Architecture 1B, where a second network is trained to drive a fixed generator, maps naturally onto motor cortex as generator with cerebellum or basal ganglia as the trainable "feedback" subsystem. Architecture 1C gives a credit-assignment scheme orthogonal to the [[Credit Assignment|dendritic lineage]]: each internal synapse is treated "as if it were the readout," receiving a **global scalar error signal** to update against its local presynaptic trace. This is a different trade from the dendritic microcircuit picture (Kording → Guerguiev → Sacramento → Payeur → Greedy → Francioni), which partitions credit spatially via compartments and interneuron predictions. FORCE's scheme partitions credit implicitly via the random feedback structure and the recurrent dynamics themselves, at the price of a biologically heroic *fast* plasticity rule. The two lineages — spatial partitioning with slower plasticity vs. global scalar error with fast plasticity — are not mutually exclusive, and the cortex-cerebellum division of labor is a natural place where both could coexist.

Third, FORCE supplies a **preparation-as-chaos-suppression** reading of [[Preparatory Activity|preparatory activity]]. In one-shot-sequence tasks, the FORCE-trained network must be initialized by a brief input pulse that drives it into a specific state from which the learned trajectory unfolds. Training reshapes this initialization such that the network's otherwise chaotic spontaneous activity collapses into a reproducible trajectory — exactly the variability-suppression signature Churchland et al. (2006) observed empirically in monkey PMd during motor preparation. The preparatory period is not merely "loading an initial condition" in an abstract sense; it is the moment when chaos in the cortical reservoir is actively suppressed and replaced by the deterministic flow that will generate the upcoming movement. This connects the [[Null Space Coding|output-null preparation]] story to the SCS chaos picture through a specific mechanism — the prepared state is the point in the reservoir from which training has carved a controlled descent.

The main biological cost of the FORCE framework is the **fast plasticity** requirement: the RLS rule updates weights on single-millisecond timescales, and the per-PC effective learning rates in the control phase are aggressive by any biological standard. Cortex likely does not implement anything this fast directly, but the framework suggests what *biologically slower* mechanisms would need to approximate: a rule that keeps errors small throughout training rather than permitting large excursions, and an error signal that can be multiplexed onto many synapses of a single neuron at once. How evolution gets the constructive result without the fast plasticity cost is the open technical problem that FORCE hands forward.

### The disordered-systems toolkit: replica methods as the static sibling of DMFT

[[parisi-1979-replica-symmetry-breaking|Parisi (1979)]] completes the analytical pair. Where [[Dynamical Mean-Field Theory|DMFT]] handles the *time-dependent* statistics of large random recurrent networks ([[sompolinsky-1988-chaos-random-networks|SCS 1988]] for asymmetric `J`, [[brunel-1999-sparsely-connected-networks|Brunel 2000]] for sparsely connected E-I spiking), [[replica-method|replica methods with hierarchical RSB]] handle the *equilibrium* statistics for symmetric `J` — the regime in which the dynamics admit an [[energy-landscape|energy function]] and converge to fixed-point attractors. Both methods reduce the `N`-body random-network problem to a tractable single-degree-of-freedom problem in the `N → ∞` limit; they are complementary halves of one analytical toolkit.

The seed result Parisi proves is that the order parameter for the mean-field [[spin-glass|Sherrington–Kirkpatrick spin glass]] cannot be a number — it must be a function `q(x)` on `[0, 1]`, encoding a hierarchical (ultrametric) organization of the system's pure states. Replica symmetry is broken; pure states sit at the leaves of a tree; pairwise overlaps between equilibrium configurations satisfy the strong triangle inequality. The function `q(x)` is the cumulative distribution of those overlaps. Convergence in the `N`-step approximation is fast — `N = 1` already gives `S(0) ≈ −0.01` against the negative-entropy paradox of the replica-symmetric Ansatz — and Talagrand (2006) eventually proved the full Parisi solution exact, 27 years after its conjecture.

For the wiki the relevance is methodological rather than directly biological — Parisi's paper makes no biological claim, but the machinery it pioneered is the analytical foundation of essentially all rigorous theory of [[attractor-dynamics|associative memory]]:

- **Hopfield-network capacity is a spin-glass transition.** Amit, Gutfreund & Sompolinsky (1985, 1987) used Parisi's framework to compute that a Hopfield network can store at most `α_c ≈ 0.138 N` random patterns before catastrophic interference. Below this bound, stored patterns are clean fixed-point attractors with sizable basins; above it, the network falls into the [[spin-glass|spin-glass phase]] where exponentially many spurious states drown the stored ones out. The capacity bound is *literally* the spin-glass transition, and [[sparse-coding|sparse codes]] raise it by reducing inter-pattern overlap — a quantitative form of the wiki's recurring theme that sparseness mitigates interference and supports fast Hebbian learning.
- **Symmetric vs. asymmetric `J` partition cleanly.** Symmetric Hopfield-style networks live in Parisi's regime: an energy function exists, the dynamics descend a (possibly rugged) [[energy-landscape|landscape]], and replica methods extract equilibrium properties. Asymmetric cortical-style networks live in the SCS regime: no energy function, chaotic flows, DMFT extracts dynamical properties. Cortical connectivity is broadly asymmetric, placing the natural cortical regime closer to SCS than to Hopfield — which is why this wiki treats spin-glass theory as a methodological foundation for associative memory rather than a direct model of cortex.
- **Rugged-landscape thinking generalizes.** The picture of an exponentially many-attractor system with hierarchical barriers has been carried (with varying degrees of justification) into discussions of deep-network loss landscapes (Choromanska et al. 2014) and of modern Hopfield architectures (Krotov–Hopfield 2016, Ramsauer 2020). In all these cases the [[replica-method|replica framework]] is the natural language for stating capacity questions like "how does typical generalization error scale with `α = p/N`."

The combined random-recurrent-network toolkit now spanning the wiki — Parisi (1979) for symmetric / static, [[sompolinsky-1988-chaos-random-networks|SCS (1988)]] for asymmetric / dynamic rate, [[brunel-1999-sparsely-connected-networks|Brunel (2000)]] for sparsely connected E-I spiking, [[wilson-cowan-1972-ei-populations|Wilson & Cowan (1972)]] for structured rate-level E-I, [[sussillo-abbott-2009-force-learning|Sussillo & Abbott (2009)]] for constructive trained dynamics — is the analytical backbone underneath the wiki's [[Computation Through Dynamics|CTD]] thread. The methods Parisi pioneered for an esoteric magnetism problem turned out to be the right language for talking about associative memory, learning capacity, and the geometry of trained recurrent networks.

### Hopfield networks: emergence as a research programme

[[hopfield-1982-collective-computational-abilities|Hopfield (1982)]] is the founding event of [[attractor-dynamics|attractor neural networks]] and the bridge that brings the [[spin-glass|spin-glass]] toolkit into theoretical neuroscience. The model itself is austere: binary neurons `V_i ∈ {0, 1}`, [[hebbian-learning|Hebbian]] outer-product storage `T_ij = Σ_s (2V_i^s − 1)(2V_j^s − 1)`, asynchronous random updates against a threshold. With symmetric `T_ij = T_ji` the dynamics admit a Lyapunov function `E = −½ Σ T_ij V_i V_j` — Hopfield identifies it explicitly as an Ising / [[spin-glass|spin-glass]] Hamiltonian and points to Kirkpatrick-Sherrington (1978) for the rugged-landscape consequences. Stored patterns become local minima of `E`; retrieval is descent down the [[energy-landscape|energy landscape]] from any starting point in a basin.

Three commitments make the paper foundational:

- **Memory is a dynamical-systems object.** Stored items as basins of attraction, retrieval as flow toward locally stable points, error correction as the basin geometry. This framing is inherited by every subsequent attractor account of memory and decision-making in the wiki — line attractors for decisions, preparatory fixed points, 1D-stable trajectories. The [[content-addressable-memory|content-addressable memory]] concept is reframed from a hardware question (how to look up by content) into a generic property of any flow with locally stable points.
- **Useful computation can be a collective property of simple parts.** The same dynamics that store memories also produce error correction, categorization, familiarity recognition, partial-cue completion, and graceful forgetting via saturation — none of which are individually engineered. This is the methodological move that puts theoretical neuroscience on the same footing as condensed-matter physics: don't design the computation, find the universality class whose collective behavior includes it. The complement to [[Degeneracy|degeneracy]]'s "many microscopic configurations, one macroscopic structure" is Hopfield's "simple substrate, rich emergent behavior."
- **The capacity bound is the spin-glass transition.** Hopfield's empirical `~0.15 N` rule of thumb is sharpened three years later by Amit, Gutfreund & Sompolinsky (1985, 1987) into `α_c ≈ 0.138 N` via Parisi's [[replica-method|replica framework]]: above this loading, stored patterns lose stability and the Hopfield network falls into a true [[spin-glass|spin-glass phase]] dominated by exponentially many spurious minima. The structured-Hebbian regime gives way to the random-coupling regime continuously as load increases. [[sparse-coding|Sparse codes]] raise the bound by reducing inter-pattern overlap — a quantitative form of the wiki's recurring theme that sparseness mitigates interference.

Hopfield's robustness studies — soft-failure under asymmetric `T`, factor-of-`2/π` capacity loss under clipping to `±1`, factor-of-`1/√2` under missing reverse synapses — anticipate the asymmetric-`J` regime that [[sompolinsky-1988-chaos-random-networks|SCS]] would analyze in detail. The Hopfield paper sits at the symmetric-equilibrium pole of the random-recurrent-network problem; SCS sits at the asymmetric-dynamic pole; together they bracket the same underlying object. The Hopfield argument for asymmetric robustness — that the energy change on a flip splits into a guaranteed-negative piece plus a stochastic piece with mean 0 — is exactly the soft bridge from the symmetric to the asymmetric regime. Cortical connectivity is broadly asymmetric, which is why the wiki treats Hopfield as methodologically central rather than directly cortical; hippocampal CA3, with its dense recurrent excitation, is the most plausible direct biological instantiation. The Hopfield network's modern descendants — dense Hopfield (Krotov-Hopfield 2016; Ramsauer 2020) achieving exponential capacity by avoiding the spin-glass regime, and the equivalence between modern Hopfield retrieval and transformer attention — extend the lineage well beyond its 1982 origins.

Under the wiki's [[overview|control-theoretic]] frame, [[content-addressable-memory|content-addressable memory]] is one of the central mechanisms by which the sensorimotor control loop extends across time and counterfactual experience. Stored attractors mean the organism does not need to recompute responses from raw sensation each time — partial cues retrieve previously-effective response patterns, and fast [[hebbian-learning|Hebbian]] writing carves new basins as new experiences accumulate. The dependence of fast Hebbian learning on [[sparse-coding|sparse codes]] ([[meister-2022-learning-fast-slow|Meister 2022]]) is exactly the content-addressable-memory perspective: sparse codes give each pattern a wide basin without overlap, making one-shot Hebbian writing geometrically clean.

### MaxEnt as the inference-theoretic foundation of the Bayesian-brain program

[[jaynes-1957-maxent-statistical-mechanics|Jaynes (1957)]] supplies the meta-theoretical backbone underneath the wiki's perception/inference thread. The claim is sweeping: statistical mechanics is a special case of statistical inference, and the [[maximum-entropy-principle|maximum-entropy distribution]] `p_i ∝ exp(−Σ_k λ_k f_k(x_i))` is the unique maximally noncommittal prior given expectation-value constraints `⟨f_k⟩`. The Boltzmann, canonical, and grand-canonical distributions drop out as special cases without ergodicity, equal a priori probabilities, or any specifically physical assumption. Temperature is just `λ = 1/kT`, the Lagrange multiplier conjugate to energy; thermodynamic and Shannon entropy are the same quantity.

This matters for the wiki because every probabilistic-brain framework already ingested rests on the inference-theoretic stance Jaynes is articulating:

- **[[variational-free-energy|Variational free energy]]** `F = ⟨E⟩_Q − H[Q]` is the Lagrangian whose stationary points are MaxEnt distributions. Free-energy minimization is, in its general form, MaxEnt inference under a constraint that `⟨E⟩` matches the data likelihood.
- **[[predictive-coding|Predictive coding]]** energy functions are MaxEnt log-priors on activities plus prediction-error terms. The Gaussian prior `g(r) = α Σ r_i²` in [[rao-ballard-1999-predictive-coding|Rao & Ballard (1999)]] is MaxEnt under a variance constraint; the kurtotic prior `g(r) = α Σ log(1+r_i²)` is MaxEnt under a kurtosis constraint, and produces sparse codes by MAP inference.
- **[[active-inference|Active inference]]** is the embodied / recurrent specialization of MaxEnt to agents that act on their environment. The dark-room objection sharpens under the MaxEnt lens: a constraint-extending agent has a *higher*-entropy prior, not a lower one, because new constraints are *added* to its registered set rather than narrowing the existing distribution. The dark-room agent has impoverished its constraint set — the opposite of what intelligence does.
- **[[sparse-coding|Sparse coding]]** and the **efficient-coding hypothesis** are MaxEnt under higher-moment constraints derived from [[natural-image-statistics|natural-image statistics]]. The convergence of sparse coding, ICA, predictive coding, and efficient coding on Gabor-like V1 RFs is unified by the observation that all four are MaxEnt inference under different but related natural-image constraints.
- **[[hopfield-network|Hopfield networks]]** and **[[energy-landscape|energy-landscape]] models** are MaxEnt under an energy constraint. The "temperature" parameter that controls retrieval dynamics is the conjugate Lagrange multiplier; energy-landscape thinking and MaxEnt inference are the same picture from different angles.

Two important caveats. First, MaxEnt determines the prior given the constraints, but says nothing about *which* constraints to register — the **constraint-discovery problem** is the deep question for adaptive intelligence and is not addressed by the framework. Building [[sparse-coding|sparse codes]] over weeks ([[reinert-2021-pfc-categorization|Reinert et al. 2021]]) or evolutionary time ([[meister-2022-learning-fast-slow|Meister 2022]]'s ecologically hardwired fear circuits) is the act of *adding* constraints; it is precisely the wiki's "extend control over more latent variables" thesis re-expressed in inference-theoretic language. Second, Jaynes is explicit that his framework is *equilibrium*: it tells us the right prior given current measurements, not the dynamics of how the system arrives there. Real brains are out-of-equilibrium dynamical systems on every timescale shorter than learning, and the right extension (max-caliber / path-MaxEnt for non-equilibrium inference) remains largely open in neural application. Jaynes therefore plays the same role in the wiki that [[parisi-1979-replica-symmetry-breaking|Parisi 1979]] plays for associative-memory capacity: a physics paper without direct biological claims, but methodologically central as the formal foundation of the theory the wiki actually relies on.

### The free-energy principle: nine global brain theories on one objective

[[friston-2010-free-energy-principle|Friston (2010)]] is the canonical statement of the [[free-energy-principle|free-energy principle (FEP)]] as a candidate **unified brain theory**. Its core thesis: any self-organizing system at non-equilibrium steady state with its environment must minimize a variational upper bound on the *surprise* (negative log evidence) of its sensory exchange. The bound is [[variational-free-energy|variational free energy]] `F`, and FEP's distinctive commitment is that **perception, action, and learning are gradient descents on the same scalar `F`** — over recognition, action, and generative variables respectively.

FEP's ambitious move is the deconstruction: nine independently-developed "global" brain theories — the Bayesian brain, hierarchical [[predictive-coding|predictive coding]] ([[rao-ballard-1999-predictive-coding|Rao-Ballard]]), [[efficient-coding-hypothesis|efficient/infomax coding]] (Barlow-Linsker-Atick), [[hebbian-learning|cell-assembly / correlation theory]] (Hebb / von der Malsburg / Singer), biased-competition / attention (Desimone-Duncan / Reynolds-Heeger), Neural Darwinism (Edelman), optimal control / RL (Sutton-Barto / Schultz / Daw-Doya), game theory / decision theory, and dynamical-systems / attractor accounts — each emerge as special cases of FEP optimization under a different read of the same `F`. Three readings of `F` (energy − entropy; surprise + KL; complexity − accuracy) are exactly equivalent, and each provides the lens for a different family of rivals.

Three placements matter for this wiki.

First, FEP is the **embodied / continuous-time generalization of the Helmholtz lineage**. The [[helmholtz-machine|Helmholtz machine]] ([[dayan-hinton-1994-helmholtz-machine|Dayan-Hinton 1994]]) optimizes free energy over recognition (φ) and generative (θ) parameters offline by [[wake-sleep-algorithm|wake-sleep]]. [[rao-ballard-1999-predictive-coding|Rao-Ballard (1999)]] specializes to continuous Gaussian latents with online gradient dynamics for vision. Friston (2010) adds action as a third variable that minimizes the same `F` — three gradient descents on one objective. This is the direct precursor of the wiki's [[active-inference|active inference]] page and the source of its "single scalar, three variables" framing.

Second, FEP makes one **concrete neural prediction** that has driven the empirical literature: **precision = synaptic gain on prediction-error units = attention**. Optimizing the inverse-variance weights `Π^(i)` of error units in the canonical-microcircuit reading (forward errors via superficial pyramidal cells; backward predictions via deep pyramidal cells) corresponds to gain modulation by neuromodulators (dopamine, ACh, NA) and by synchrony. This connects FEP directly to [[divisive-normalization|divisive normalization]] (Reynolds-Heeger normalization as the FEP-optimal gain mechanism), to biased-competition accounts of attention, and to the aberrant-precision reading of schizophrenia symptoms (Adams-Stephan-Brown 2013). It is the one place where FEP's claims are sharply testable against existing electrophysiology — and recent mouse-V1 work (Keller-Mrsic-Flogel 2018; Attinger 2017; Zmarz-Keller 2016) has begun to probe the prediction-error layer of the canonical microcircuit, though the precision-as-gain claim itself has not been directly tested.

Third, FEP's **resolution of the [[dark-room-problem|dark-room problem]]** — agents avoid surprising states under heritable phenotype-specific priors — relocates the substantive question from "why does the agent move" to "where do the priors come from." This is the **constraint-discovery problem** in the embodied / evolutionary setting: the same one Jaynes flagged for MaxEnt, transposed onto an agent embedded in an environment. FEP says agents minimize free energy under the priors they have; it does not say where those priors come from. The control-theoretic reading of [[Degeneracy]] in this wiki — that genes specify nonlinear feedback control algorithms whose conserved limit-cycle attractors produce species-typical behavior with high probability despite microscopic configuration — is one candidate answer; FEP itself is silent on it.

Two ways FEP **falls short of the wiki's organizing thesis**, both relevant to subsequent ingest decisions.

First, **architecture**: most active-inference implementations require modular structure (separate prior specification, recognition, action selection). [[li-2024-prediction-noise-reward|Li et al. (2024) PaN]] is positioned explicitly against this: it replaces FEP's prior + recognition + action triad with prediction + noise, getting closed-loop reward-seeking from instability of the dark-room state without a planner. The two routes to closed-loop control under prediction error — heritable priors (FEP) and noise-induced instability (PaN) — are not exclusive: priors set the attractor landscape, noise prevents collapse to trivial fixed points. The wiki's PaN section already documented this contrast; Friston (2010) is the canonical statement of the framework PaN is contrasted with.

Second, **constraint discovery**: FEP delegates the prior-specification problem to natural selection without further specification. The wiki's evolutionary thesis offers a candidate mechanism (genes shape neurons' nonlinear feedback control algorithms; conserved macroscopic attractors emerge with high probability), but FEP does not commit to it. Reconciliation between FEP, Neural Darwinism, and the genes→control→structure hypothesis remains open.

The relationship between FEP and [[computation-through-dynamics|CTD]] is also unresolved. CTD-style trained recurrent dynamics could in principle be derived as FEP minimizers under priors on hidden-state trajectories, but CTD is largely silent on generative models and FEP is largely silent on the specific dynamical motifs (rotations, line attractors, fixed-point initialization) that CTD catalogues. Whether the two frameworks compose, contradict, or operate at different conceptual layers is one of the cleaner open questions the wiki has now articulated.

### Memory's molecular substrate predates the nervous system

[[kukushkin-2024-massed-spaced-non-neural|Kukushkin, Carney, Tabassum & Carew (2024)]] is the cleanest demonstration to date that the canonical features of memory — temporal pattern detection, transcriptional consolidation, Ebbinghaus-style forgetting curves — are not unique to neurons. They are properties of conserved cellular signaling cascades that exist across many cell types. Two human cell lines, SH-SY5Y (neuroblastoma) and HEK293 (embryonic kidney, with no neural ancestry), reproduce the canonical [[spacing-effect|massed-spaced learning effect]] when "trained" with temporally patterned pulses of forskolin (PKA activator) and TPA (PKC activator) and read out with a destabilized PEST-tagged firefly luciferase under a CRE promoter. Four 3-min pulses at ITI = 10–20 min produce roughly 3.9-fold higher luciferase at 24 h than a single equivalent pulse, and the effect is blocked by pharmacological inhibition of either ERK (U0126) or [[creb|CREB]] (666-15).

The result divides cleanly: the **temporal-pattern decoding and transcriptional consolidation** components of memory live in cellular signaling cascades; the **pairwise activity-dependent specificity** (which connection changes given correlated pre/post activity) lives at synapses. Synaptic plasticity is therefore a *combination* of two ingredients — one cellular, one synaptic — that the wiki had been treating as a single phenomenon. Late-phase [[long-term-potentiation|LTP]] inherits its [[spacing-effect|spacing effect]] from cellular cascade kinetics; the synapse adds the pairwise term that selects which connection changes.

This reframes the wiki's evolutionary thesis. **Genes encode behavior in part by encoding the kinetics of cellular signaling cascades** that detect temporal patterns. PKA decay rates, PKC activation thresholds, ERK phosphorylation/dephosphorylation balance, CREB phosphorylation half-life, and the gain of CREB → BDNF → ERK positive feedback loops are all evolvable parameters — and small changes to them produce large changes in which temporal patterns the cell (and therefore the organism) treats as memorable. The control-theoretic reading of [[Degeneracy]] extends naturally to the cellular level: cellular cascades are nonlinear feedback control systems, and the kinetic parameters genes select among shape what patterns the cell can compute.

Two specific consequences for the wiki:

**Two sources of slow learning.** [[meister-2022-learning-fast-slow|Meister (2022)]]'s framing — fast Hebbian plasticity but slow representation-building — needs an addendum. Even at the *non-synaptic* single-cell level, transcriptional consolidation is slow (24-h kinetics in Kukushkin's CRE-luc system). Some of the slow timescale of mammalian learning is genuinely *molecular* rather than representational. The two contributions to slowness — slow construction of sparse codes (Meister) and slow molecular consolidation (Kukushkin) — are not exclusive; both are at work.

**A cellular floor under the cognitive stack.** The wiki's organizing thesis builds intelligence from closed-loop sensorimotor control by adding machinery that extends the loop's reach (memory across time, sparse codes across latent variables, planning across counterfactuals). [[cellular-cognition|Cellular cognition]] supplies the molecular floor: even before synapses, before circuits, before nervous systems, single cells already had the temporal-pattern-decoding and consolidation machinery that any memory system must build on. The progression from this floor — cellular cascades → synaptic plasticity → circuit-level associative memory → systems consolidation → cognitive map construction → planning — is a clean ladder of mechanisms each of which inherits from the level below. PKA / PKC / ERK / CREB are at the bottom rung. Hippocampal SWR-driven systems consolidation (Wierzynski 2009) is several rungs up, operating on whatever synaptic changes the cellular consolidation step has stabilized.

The framework also clarifies what synapses uniquely contribute. The synapse-uniqueness claim from earlier wiki sources (e.g., that no non-neural system has activity-dependent plasticity) requires careful refinement: synapses uniquely provide *pairwise* activity-dependent plasticity (which-connection specificity), but the temporal-pattern-decoding plasticity that supports the spacing effect is reproducible in non-neural cells. This sharpening matters for thinking about what evolutionary novelty the nervous system introduced — not the molecular machinery of memory, which it inherited, but the network architecture and pairwise-coincidence-detecting synapse that lets cellular memory primitives be deployed at the level of specific connections in a graph.

### Methodological skepticism: the bottleneck is methods, not data

[[jonas-2017-microprocessor-critique|Jonas & Kording (2017)]] put a sharp methodological-philosophical anchor into the wiki. They take a fully understood artificial system — the MOS 6502 microprocessor with its 3,510-transistor netlist, every wire and every transistor state simulable, and three different "behaviors" (Donkey Kong, Space Invaders, Pitfall) — and apply the dominant analytical methods of contemporary systems neuroscience: connectomics-style cell-type discovery, lesion studies, single-unit tuning curves, spike-word correlations, LFP / power-spectrum analysis, Granger causality, and NMF-based dimensionality reduction. Each method produces results that look superficially like results from real brains. None of them recovers what we already know about how the chip computes.

The framework that makes "what we already know" precise is [[marrs-levels-of-analysis|Marr's three levels]] (computational / algorithmic / implementational), now an explicit benchmark in the wiki for what understanding looks like, plus Lazebnik's "can you fix it?" test as the operational version. Standard methods recover the implementational level (cell-type clusters, lesion-effect maps, tuning, correlations, latent dimensions) but rarely the algorithmic or computational level — even when ground truth is available.

The implications cut across several wiki threads. **For [[degeneracy|degeneracy]]**: the chip has only one physical transistor type yet recovers many "cell types" by connectivity-based clustering — the inverse of biological degeneracy (different microscopic implementations → same macroscopic function), but with the same conclusion that structure → function is not invertible by current methods. **For [[computation-through-dynamics|CTD]] and [[neural-manifolds|neural manifolds]]**: NMF recovers six latent dimensions cleanly correlated with the chip's clock and read-write line — exactly the form of result that motor-cortex and PFC dim-reduction analyses produce — without recovering the chip's computational hierarchy. The strongest defense is the trained-RNN sufficiency proof ([[sussillo-abbott-2009-force-learning|FORCE]]-style demonstration that a model with the recovered structure actually generates the behavior); for analyses that stop at correlation, the chip-NMF result is a direct caution. **For [[edge-of-chaos|criticality]]**: the chip produces 1/f-like LFP spectra without being near any computational critical state, so spectral signatures alone do not establish criticality and require independent mechanistic anchors (the SCS / Brunel finite-size coherence-time scaling provides one). **For tuning-based inferences** (V1 receptive fields, [[place-cells|place cells]], category cells): apparent tuning to a quantity does not entail involvement in computing that quantity — chip transistors strongly tuned to pixel luminance are not in the luminance computation. Constructive mechanistic accounts of tuning (e.g., the [[zhang-2024-endotaxis-neuromorphic-navigation|endotaxis circuit]] proposal for place fields) are what distinguish computational claims from correlational ones. **For lesion studies**: behavioral specificity from a focal perturbation reveals which generic operations a particular behavior happens to require, not the computational specialization of the perturbed element.

The paper's most consequential claim is the negative one: **data scarcity is not the bottleneck.** The processor is the limit of data availability — every internal state, on every clock cycle, with full perturbation control. Methods still fall short. Inferring that "more data" will solve neuroscience is therefore unsupported on the methods side; the marginal value of data is bounded by the analyses being applied to it. The constructive proposal is that ground-truth-anchored validation against engineered systems should be a precondition for trusting an analytical method on neural data — the way bioinformatics methods are validated on synthetic genomes before being applied to biology. This methodological discipline is, the paper implies, a missing piece of normal science in systems neuroscience.

For the wiki's organizing thesis, the paper acts as a calibrating constraint rather than a new structural claim. The claim that intelligence begins with closed-loop sensorimotor control is not a methods claim and is not directly affected. But the empirical evidence the wiki adduces for that thesis — manifold structure in motor cortex, attractor structure in *C. elegans*, scaffold structure in PFC, latent dimensions correlated with task variables — all rests on methods that the chip experiment partially undercuts. The right response is not to discard the evidence but to demand stronger sufficiency proofs and cross-system transferability. CTD's trained-RNN existence proofs, [[brennan-2023-looper-computational-scaffold|LOOPER]]'s counterfactual-prediction tests, and the dendritic credit-assignment lineage's experimental closure ([[francioni-2026-vectorized-dendritic-signals|Francioni 2026]]) are the templates for what stronger evidence looks like.

### Coding as misleading metaphor — Brette's negative argument and the wiki's positive program

[[brette-2018-coding-metaphor|Brette (2018)]] is the most thorough sustained philosophical critique of the [[neural-coding|neural coding metaphor]] in the contemporary literature. It is also the most explicit articulation, in the literature, of the framework the wiki has adopted as its organizing principle. The two halves are mutually supporting: Brette's negative argument — that neural coding cannot be the basis for theories of brain function — and the wiki's positive thesis that intelligence begins with closed-loop sensorimotor control are flip sides of the same coin.

Brette's critique has three load-bearing arguments, each addressing a distinct sense of the coding metaphor (after Perkel & Bullock 1968):

**Representational power is weaker than implied.** Tuning curves establish *sensitivity* to a stimulus parameter when other parameters are held fixed; they do not establish that the cell carries information about that parameter in any other context. Crick's (1979) "fallacy of the overwise neuron" shows this for cones: a cone's response varies with wavelength when intensity is held fixed, but animals with single-cone retinas are color-blind because the same response is consistent with many wavelength-intensity combinations. The same context-dependence holds for V1 orientation tuning (Bolz & Gilbert 1986; Pakan 2018; Chanauria 2018), MSO/IC ITD tuning (Brette 2010, 2018; Goodman et al. 2013), and Bayesian-brain readouts of population codes (Knill & Pouget 2004). The "ideal observer" critique (Skottun 1998) sharpens this: comparing single-neuron sensitivity to behavioral discrimination via an ideal observer requires an unjustified linking proposition (Teller 1984) — that the brain reads the code the same way the experimenter does.

**Codes carry information by reference, not by structure.** Shannon information is correspondence to a *known external message*. The experimenter has access to the message; the brain does not. This is the [[neural-coding|symbol grounding problem]] (Harnad 1990) applied to neural representations. The **efficient-coding paradox**: a maximally efficient code (Barlow 1961; Olshausen & Field 2004) minimizes redundancy and is therefore statistically indistinguishable from random noise — *unreadable* by any reader without access to the input distribution. The notion of information implied by efficient coding is therefore information accessible only to external observers (the experimenter, evolution), not the kind of information accessible to the brain from neural activity alone. The right notion of information for an organism is the *invariant structure of relations among its sensor signals and actions* — Gibson's (1986) ecological invariants, [[sensorimotor-contingencies|O'Regan & Noë's (2001) sensorimotor contingencies]], Brette's [[subjective-physics|subjective physics]]. Information lives in *laws*: "if I do action A, then sensory property B happens." This is **information by structure**, directly verifiable by the organism through its own actions.

**Causal structure is incongruent with the brain's.** Coding describes brain function as a linear chain (encode → transform → decode → effector — "dominoes"). The brain's actual causal structure is circular: a distributed dynamical system mutually coupled to itself and to the environment ("tent"). The two structures are categorically different. The Paramecium "swimming neuron" example: a single excitable cell's tuning to chemical concentration is consistent with two analyses — efficient-coding (set by input statistics) or sensorimotor-loop (set by the closed control loop that produces effective chemotaxis) — that make different empirical predictions. Real organisms behave according to the second; the field's analytical tools produce results consistent with the first. The cardiac cells example: rate-coding analysis would conclude the heart "uses a rate code" for running speed, yet the synchrony of atrial vs. ventricular contraction is *life-critical*. A coding analysis would correctly characterize input-output sensitivity but completely miss the causal structure that matters for function. Coding variables (firing rates averaged over windows, BOLD signals, trial-averaged spike counts) are aggregate observables, not state variables of the brain's dynamical system; only spike events have causal powers, and they are *transitions* in state space, not coding-variable values.

For the wiki's organizing principle, Brette is uniquely load-bearing. Three commitments inherited:
- **The substrate for intelligence is the closed sensorimotor loop**, not a perceptual encoder feeding a cognitive symbol manipulator. [[motor-cortex|Motor cortex]] is the canonical case, where CTD-style trained dynamics ([[vyas-2020-computation-through-dynamics|Vyas 2020]]; [[sussillo-abbott-2009-force-learning|Sussillo & Abbott 2009]]) are exactly the "models that behave" Brette argues should replace coding-style decomposition.
- **Information for the organism is relational structure**. The [[subjective-physics|subjective physics]] framework specifies the right unit of analysis: lawful relations among observables and actions. The Martian iguana example makes this concrete — head movement transforms what would be Shannon-meaningless acoustic patterns into a manipulable internal sensorimotor model that *defines* spatial position for the iguana, not as an external property but as a counterfactual to be computed within the model.
- **Spikes are timed actions, not symbols**. Action potentials produce actions; aggregate coding variables (rate averages, BOLD, population probabilities) lack causal powers. This is consistent with the wiki's existing framing of [[neural-noise|neural noise]] but explicit only after Brette's argument.

The critique recontextualizes several Bayesian-brain-lineage frameworks the wiki tracks. [[predictive-coding|Predictive coding]] retains the generative-model framing where latents map causes → observables; the latents face the symbol grounding problem. [[active-inference|Active inference]] partially addresses the static-coding problem (action is in the loop) but retains the generative-model framing. [[free-energy-principle|FEP]] inherits the coding-metaphor structure at the principle level. The [[helmholtz-machine|Helmholtz machine]] *enforces* the encoder/decoder dualism Brette diagnoses as Cartesian. [[sparse-coding|Sparse coding]] and the [[efficient-coding-hypothesis|efficient coding hypothesis]] face the efficient-coding paradox directly. In each case, the *mechanistic* content (cortical microcircuits, dendritic compartments, plasticity rules, BCS-style trained dynamics) survives reinterpretation; the *representational* content is contested.

The critique aligns with several already-established threads. [[computation-through-dynamics|CTD]] is largely consistent with Brette's positive program — neural populations as dynamical systems whose evolution *is* the computation, trained RNNs as hypothesis generators, fixed-point analysis as a tool for understanding flow rather than for extracting codes. [[self-supervised-behavior|Self-supervised behavior]] / [[li-2024-prediction-noise-reward|Li et al. (2024)]] fits the frame: emergent goal-seeking from local dynamics + noise, no external reward signal, no encode/decode dualism. [[ethological-paradigms|Ethological paradigms]] / [[rosenberg-2021-labyrinth-learning|Rosenberg et al. (2021)]] is directly aligned: trial-based paradigms are the conditions under which coding-style descriptions look most plausible (everything else held fixed), and naturalistic paradigms expose the context-dependence that makes them fail. [[brennan-2019-conserved-macroscopic-dynamics|Brennan & Proekt]], [[banerjee-2023-life-stage-chemosensation|Banerjee et al.]], and the [[degeneracy|degeneracy]] thread more broadly all point at circuit-behavior mappings that coding-style descriptions cannot accommodate. [[zhang-2024-endotaxis-neuromorphic-navigation|Zhang et al. 2024]]'s endotaxis circuit is the cleanest worked example of a "model that behaves" at cortical scale: ancient chemotaxis controller extended by an internal signal generator, with map-cell connectivity learning the *adjacency structure* of the environment as a relational object — the kind of thing subjective physics would specify.

Brette is also the philosophical companion to [[jonas-2017-microprocessor-critique|Jonas & Kording (2017)]]. The two papers form a two-front methodological-philosophical critique. Jonas-Kording: standard analyses don't recover [[marrs-levels-of-analysis|Marr-level]] understanding even with full ground truth on a known artificial system. Brette: even where those analyses converge cleanly, the metaphor that gives them representational meaning is incoherent. Together they call for ground-truth-anchored validation of methods *and* a reframing of theory in terms of [[subjective-physics|subjective physics]] / [[sensorimotor-contingencies|sensorimotor contingencies]]. The wiki's stance — coding-flavored language used because it is the literature's vocabulary, but read as correspondence claims with experimental scope rather than as representational entailments — is the constructive synthesis of both critiques.

The major open question Brette opens is the implementation question: subjective physics specifies *what kind* of model the brain has but is largely silent on neural implementation. Synchrony receptive fields ([[binding-problem]]) are a candidate primitive but only for symmetric (equality-flavored) relations; what carries directed and structured relations (the "Paul drives car" problem) remains open. The dendritic credit-assignment lineage ([[credit-assignment]]) admits a sensorimotor reading where the apical-compartment teaching signal is residual sensorimotor mismatch routed for local plasticity, but the formal mapping has not been worked out. These are the load-bearing open questions for the wiki's program going forward.

### Principles vs parameters — what is communicable about a learned brain

[[lillicrap-kording-2019-understanding-neural-networks|Lillicrap & Kording (2019)]] supply the constructive third leg of the wiki's methodological-philosophical thread. [[jonas-2017-microprocessor-critique|Jonas & Kording (2017)]] showed that standard methods fail to recover [[marrs-levels-of-analysis|Marr-level]] understanding even with full ground truth; [[brette-2018-coding-metaphor|Brette (2018)]] showed that the coding framework that interprets such methods is incoherent as a basis for theories of brain function. Lillicrap-Kording 2019 says we should *expect* the negative results and reorganizes the program: focus on the *recipe* that builds a brain (architecture, plasticity rules, developmental program, [[credit-assignment|credit-assignment]] machinery) rather than on the *trained parameters* the recipe produces in any individual animal.

The argument turns on **compressibility**. Tic-tac-toe (255,168 games) admits a three-rule unbeatable policy; Go (>10¹⁰⁴⁸ games) admits no compact valuation. Human-level performance across many domains lives at the Go end because the world the system models is itself incompressible: humans encode thousands of object categories with megabytes of incompressible per-category content (the "balloons are red in a famous song" example) and ~1.5 MB of language-acquisition information. The trained synaptic state of an adult brain reflects this incompressibility and cannot be communicated in any short formal language. What *can* be communicated is bounded: the genome upper-bounds the principles at ~6 × 10⁹ bits (mostly non-coding, ~20k coding genes), enough for plasticity rules + architecture + developmental program but not the trained state. The principles are compressible by construction; the parameters are not.

The decomposition is the **information-theoretic complement of the wiki's [[degeneracy|control-theoretic genes hypothesis]]**: genes encode behavior not by specifying microscopic circuits but by modifying neurons' nonlinear feedback control algorithms, producing conserved macroscopic dynamical structures (limit-cycle attractors for locomotion, etc.) with high probability despite unique microscopic configurations. The control-theoretic argument says *what* genes specify (dynamics, not circuits); Lillicrap-Kording supplies the information-theoretic argument for *why* it must be so (the bounded recipe is the only thing the genome can specify; the unbounded trained state is what the world fills in). The conserved macroscopic structures degeneracy makes visible *are* the principles.

For [[marrs-levels-of-analysis|Marr's framework]] the recasting is sharp: the algorithmic level for a trained system performing across an incompressible world is *not* a separately summarizable layer. It is the trained parameters running on the architecture. What is communicable is the computational level (the objective), the implementational level (the substrate), and the *recipe* — architecture + loss + learning rule + developmental program — that converts implementation + world into algorithm. The trained algorithm is an output of the recipe, not a layer of explanation. This is captured on the [[principles-vs-parameters|principles-vs-parameters]] page.

Methodologically the program is concrete and reorganizes existing wiki threads:
- **Connectomics**: focus on circuit motifs (genetically specified) rather than exact wiring (learned).
- **Plasticity research**: the dendritic credit-assignment lineage ([[kording-konig-2001-two-integration-sites|Kording & Konig 2001]] → [[guerguiev-2017-segregated-dendrites|Guerguiev 2017]] → [[sacramento-2018-dendritic-microcircuits-backprop|Sacramento 2018]] → [[payeur-2020-burst-dependent-credit-assignment|Payeur 2020]] → [[greedy-2022-single-phase-burstccn|Greedy 2022]] → [[francioni-2026-vectorized-dendritic-signals|Francioni 2026]]) is paradigmatic principles-level research — the contribution is the learning rule, not any particular trained representation.
- **[[force-learning|FORCE learning]]** ([[sussillo-abbott-2009-force-learning|Sussillo & Abbott 2009]]) likewise: the contribution is the learning architecture, not particular trained networks.
- **Cellular cognition** ([[kukushkin-2024-massed-spaced-non-neural|Kukushkin 2024]]): isolating CREB-driven temporal-pattern decoding kinetics is principles-style research — the cascade is the recipe, the resulting transcription is the parameter.
- **[[predictive-coding|Predictive coding]] / [[free-energy-principle|FEP]]**: principles-level theories specifying objectives and architectures; the dark-room / constraint-discovery problems are the parameters questions the frame predicts will be hard.
- **[[computation-through-dynamics|CTD]]**: the strongest reading (recovered dynamics *are* the explanation) is in tension with the frame; the CTD-compatible response is that dynamics are properties of the recipe (architecture + loss + training regime) rather than of any particular trained instantiation, with the trained-RNN sufficiency move as the load-bearing defense.

The **slogan**: instead of asking *how the brain works*, ask *how the brain learns to work*. For the wiki this is a methodological refinement of the organizing thesis. Closed-loop sensorimotor control is the substantive primitive (what the brain is for), and principles-vs-parameters is the methodological complement (what neuroscience can hope to communicate). The dendritic credit-assignment lineage, FORCE learning, the cellular-cognition thread, and the dynamical-systems substrate ([[sompolinsky-1988-chaos-random-networks|SCS]], [[brunel-1999-sparsely-connected-networks|Brunel]], [[wilson-cowan-1972-ei-populations|Wilson-Cowan]]) all read as *principles*-level commitments under the frame; the parameters layer (specific tunings, specific synaptic states) is for measurement and constraint, not for the load-bearing object of explanation.

### Paradigm pluralism — comp neuro as a multi-paradigm ecology

[[chatgpt-2026-comp-neuro-kuhnian-review|ChatGPT (2026)]] (an LLM-generated deep research report, ingested as navigation document with explicit provenance flags) supplies the *meta-frame* the wiki had been operating in implicitly. Computational neuroscience is best read not as a single Kuhnian normal science with paradigm shifts but as a **stable multi-paradigm ecology**: nine paradigm clusters (single-neuron biophysics, rate-based networks, spiking networks, Bayesian/probabilistic coding, predictive coding, deep learning, reinforcement learning, dynamical systems / attractors, connectomics) each with their own canonical exemplars, methods, and inherited evaluative standards. The same dataset can be a "success" by predictive-accuracy standards (deep learning) and an "anomaly" by explanatory-adequacy standards (cognitive psychophysics) — Kuhnian incommensurability of standards.

Two adaptations of Kuhn matter for the field:
- **[[marrs-levels-of-analysis|Marr's levels]]** defuse many apparent paradigm conflicts as level-mismatches (biophysics targets implementation; rate / spiking / dynamical-systems target algorithm; Bayesian / predictive coding / RL target computation). Two paradigms not aimed at the same level are complementary, not rival.
- **Inherited standards differ across paradigms** — biophysical realism, predictive accuracy, normative optimality, behavioral fit, engineering performance — generating *genuine* Kuhnian conflicts when paradigms target the same level with different success criteria. The load-bearing examples are rate-vs-timing in [[neural-coding|neural codes]], Bayesian optimality vs. constraint-based suboptimality, expectation-as-prediction vs. adaptation in predictive coding, predictive accuracy vs. explanatory adequacy in DNN-as-brain-model, and structure-first connectomics vs. dynamics-first explanations.

The diagnosis is **localized crisis signals, no field-wide crisis**: Bayesian optimality has faced "just-so story" critiques and major reviews questioning falsifiability; DNN-as-brain-model has faced target-article critiques in BBS-style venues (Bowers et al. 2023); the connectomics-to-understanding optimism is explicitly problematized by [[jonas-2017-microprocessor-critique|Jonas & Kording 2017]]; the dopamine/RL program is patching with multi-factor accounts rather than abandoning a scalar RPE; the predictive-coding program faces the recurring expectation-vs-adaptation tension; the dynamical-systems / attractor paradigm faces the activity-silent working memory anomaly. None of these aggregates into a field-wide crisis because the field has no shared dominant paradigm whose loss would constitute one. The aggregate pattern is **proliferation plus hybridization** — benchmarking platforms, hybrid models, integrative-discipline calls — consistent with research-program expansion rather than Kuhnian revolution.

For the wiki this is a calibrating constraint rather than a structural change. The wiki's organizing thesis (closed-loop sensorimotor control as substrate for intelligence) is itself one paradigm-style commitment among several — a *normative bet* on which paradigm best supports a biologically grounded route to understanding intelligence, not a settled fact of the field. The methodological-philosophical triad already in the wiki ([[brette-2018-coding-metaphor|Brette]] — coding metaphor incoherent; [[jonas-2017-microprocessor-critique|Jonas-Kording]] — methods don't recover Marr-level understanding; [[lillicrap-kording-2019-understanding-neural-networks|Lillicrap-Kording]] — parameters not communicable, target the recipe) is itself a paradigm-internal stance, sharing standards across the three but contested by paradigms with different standards (biophysical-realism, predictive-accuracy, normative-optimality). The Kuhnian frame recommends reading rival paradigms on their own terms while looking for productive *paradigm-pair interfaces* — CTD × connectomics, predictive coding × CTD, multi-factor dopamine × CTD, Bayesian × efficient coding — as the live frontier rather than expecting a single successor paradigm to absorb the field.
